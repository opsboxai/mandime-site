An explosion at Anduril’s rocket motor test site in Mississippi last Friday is a serious setback for the company’s ambitious plans. No one was hurt, but the blast damaged the testing stand, halting a key part of Anduril’s rocket motor business. Anduril’s COO, Matt Grimm, confirmed the incident and said they expect to resume testing within weeks. However, insiders suggest that rebuilding could take up to a couple of months, and the company’s mass production plans, originally slated for July 1, 2025, may face further delays.

Anduril has been valued at $61 billion, but its rocket motor unit has seen its fair share of challenges. In March, WIRED reported on safety and technology issues at the McHenry facility, including an employee burning his hand and costly equipment failing to perform as intended. The company has been generating revenue by designing and testing prototype motors for customers like the US Navy, but recent setbacks have led to declining morale and employee departures.

Despite Anduril’s optimistic statements, insiders describe the situation as a “hot mess,” with the company gutting and starting over on what they had spent years developing. The explosion and subsequent delays could disrupt Anduril’s plans to become a major supplier of missile propulsion systems, and it remains to be seen if they can recover and meet their ambitious goals.
