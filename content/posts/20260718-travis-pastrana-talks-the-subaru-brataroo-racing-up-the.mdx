Travis Pastrana just took a lifted, turbocharged Subaru Brat up a narrow British hill at speed, and somehow that's not even the weirdest thing he's done this month. The Brataroo, as it's actually called, is exactly what it sounds like: a 1985 Brat with a modern Subaru boxer engine, rally suspension geometry, and enough horsepower to make a sensible person question every decision that led to this moment. It exists because Pastrana saw a gap in the world where a homemade rally weapon wearing a 40-year-old body should be, and he filled it.

What makes this worth paying attention to isn't just that he drove it up Goodwood. It's that Pastrana built something that feels genuinely personal in a world where most "one-off" vehicles are either sponsored billionaire projects or YouTube content plays. The Brataroo isn't trying to be the fastest or the most expensive. It's trying to be the most fun, and there's a real difference. He's thinking about the actual experience of driving a thing, not the metrics or the Instagram moment. That's the thread that connects every project he's touched since his early days in rally.

The Ken Block connection matters here too. Block was pushing rally into the mainstream through precision, sponsorship, and yes, the spectacle of it all. Pastrana's approach sits somewhere else. He's reminding people that you don't need a seven-figure budget or a global energy drink deal to build something that moves. You just need to understand what makes a car feel alive and have the skills to prove it works. The Brataroo is that argument in metal and rubber.
