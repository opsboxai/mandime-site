OpenAI is in for a rough ride, and it's not just because of the usual suspects like Elon Musk. Last Friday, Apple dropped a bombshell, filing a lawsuit accusing OpenAI of stealing trade secrets from its former employees. This isn't just another lawsuit; it's a high-stakes game that could derail OpenAI's ambitious hardware plans.

The lawsuit centers around three former Apple employees who now work at OpenAI. Apple claims these individuals stole confidential information about hardware development, manufacturing, and supply chain operations. The stakes are sky-high, with Apple arguing that these trade secrets are among the most valuable assets in American business. The allegations are wild, including claims that one of the accused, Tang Tan, encouraged prospective hires to bring Apple hardware out of the office for "show and tell" during interviews.

This lawsuit couldn't come at a worse time for OpenAI. The company is preparing to go public, facing pressure to turn a profit, and is set to release a hardware device in 2027 after a massive $6.5 billion acquisition of Jony Ive's hardware startup, io. The legal battle with Apple could be a costly distraction, especially since Apple is known for being a tenacious litigant.

Sam Altman, OpenAI's CEO, may not be afraid of Elon Musk, but he's clearly wary of Apple. The lawsuit represents a significant departure from the previously positive relationship between the two tech giants. As the AI industry becomes more competitive, these kinds of legal skirmishes are becoming increasingly common. OpenAI's hardware ambitions are on the line, and the outcome of this lawsuit could determine whether the company can stay ahead in the race to dominate AI hardware.
