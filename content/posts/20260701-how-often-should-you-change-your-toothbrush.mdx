Turns out, your toothbrush might be older than you think. Most guys don’t give it a second thought until the bristles look like they’ve seen better days. But here’s the deal: you should be swapping out that trusty old toothbrush every three to four months. That’s right, it’s time to upgrade more frequently than you probably thought.

The condition of your bristles is key. Once they start to splay outward, they’re not cleaning as effectively as they should. Dr. Arthur Glosman, a cosmetic dentist in Beverly Hills, points out that worn bristles can’t sweep plaque away from your teeth and gumline as well. This means you’re at a higher risk for bad breath, cavities, and gum disease. And if you’ve been sick, it’s a no-brainer to swap out your toothbrush to avoid reintroducing those pesky bacteria into your mouth.

Think about it: a new toothbrush costs less than a morning coffee. It’s a small investment that can make a big difference in your oral health. So set a reminder, and don’t wait until your dentist hands you a free one every six months. Your teeth and gums will thank you.
