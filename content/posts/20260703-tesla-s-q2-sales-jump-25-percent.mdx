Tesla just reported a 25 percent jump in Q2 sales, delivering 480,126 vehicles. That's a significant rebound from the brutal sales year in 2025. The company produced 451,758 vehicles in the quarter, with the Model 3 and Model Y leading the charge, accounting for 442,936 units. The rest of the production was split between the Cybertruck and Tesla Semi, with 8,822 units produced.

This surge in production and delivery numbers is a clear sign that Tesla is getting back on track after a tough year. The increase from 384,122 vehicles delivered in Q2 of 2025 to 480,126 this year is a solid win, especially considering Wall Street was only expecting around 400,000 to 420,000 vehicles. 

But it's not all smooth sailing. Tesla is still grappling with safety concerns around its Full Self-Driving (FSD) technology. A recent fatal crash involving a Tesla and a home has sparked investigations and lawsuits, casting a shadow over the company's ambitious autonomous driving goals. Meanwhile, Tesla's robotaxi service is operating at a much smaller scale than originally promised, with only about 60 to 70 Model Y vehicles in operation across a few Texas cities.

Despite these challenges, the numbers are undeniably impressive. Tesla's ability to ramp up production and meet growing demand shows that the company is still a force to be reckoned with in the electric vehicle market.
