A 400-pound boulder pinned a firefighter's leg on North Apostle Mountain, but that wasn't the end of the story. The firefighter, with a mix of "incredible determination" and "primal adrenaline," managed to push the boulder off and free themselves. But with a broken leg and no way to get down, they called for help, setting off a complex rescue operation.

A Black Hawk helicopter from a High-Altitude Army Aviation Training Site (HAATS) was deployed, thanks to the remote location and steep terrain. The firefighter used the emergency texting feature on their iPhone to send location info to Vail Mountain Rescue, who relayed it to the helicopter crew. Two technical rescuers from Mountain Rescue Aspen's hoist team were lowered via a 300-foot hoist line, splinted the broken leg, and safely hoisted the injured person into the helicopter.

What makes this story even more remarkable is that one of the helicopter pilots was completing his final mission before retirement. After the rescue, he received a traditional military honor with a water cannon salute over the helicopter at the Eagle County Airport Fire Rescue.

This rescue is a testament to the incredible teamwork and skill of first responders. It's a reminder that even those who dedicate their lives to helping others sometimes need help themselves.
