LastPass users, brace yourselves: the password manager has been hit by yet another data breach. This time, it's not a direct hit on LastPass's own infrastructure, but rather a compromise at Klue, an AI business intelligence firm. The attackers managed to grab access tokens from Klue, which they then used to siphon off data from Salesforce and other integrated platforms. The stolen data includes names, phone numbers, email addresses, physical addresses, and support case data. 

While LastPass insists that password vaults were not affected, this breach highlights the growing complexity of data security in a world where multiple services are interconnected. The company's advice to stay vigilant against phishing and social engineering attempts is sound, but it's a reminder that no system is entirely foolproof. As breaches continue to occur, it's crucial to diversify your security measures, including using two-factor authentication and regularly updating your passwords. 

Meanwhile, former national security adviser John Bolton pleaded guilty to mishandling classified information, agreeing to a plea deal that could spare him prison time. The case underscores the ongoing tension between national security and the handling of sensitive information, especially as more former officials face scrutiny for their actions. 

On a brighter note, Microsoft and Europol teamed up to disrupt the infrastructure of major infostealers like Amadey and StealC, recovering up to 27 million stolen access credentials and flagging about $47 million worth of stolen cryptocurrency. This operation shows that while cyber threats are evolving, so are the methods to combat them. 

Stay vigilant, folks. The digital landscape is a minefield, and it's up to us to navigate it safely.
