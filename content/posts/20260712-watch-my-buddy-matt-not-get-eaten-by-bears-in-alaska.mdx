Matt Addington, the Minnesota-based photographer with a knack for capturing stunning outdoor moments, recently found himself face-to-face with a couple of bears in Alaska. The video he shot is going viral for a reason: it's damn close. 

Addington and his team were in Katmai National Park, where bears are known to get up close and personal, thanks to the abundance of food and strict visitor management. But let's be clear: this isn't a DIY project. These bears were about 100 yards away when Addington and his crew set up, and they stayed put, letting the bears come to them. 

Professional guides Scott and Jackie Stone were on hand, ensuring that Addington's encounter was as safe as it was thrilling. If you're thinking about trying this yourself, don't. Just don't. The stakes are way too high, and you'll end up as bear chow. Stick to guided tours if you want to get that close to nature. 

And yes, we're all hoping he wore his brown pants under his waders.
