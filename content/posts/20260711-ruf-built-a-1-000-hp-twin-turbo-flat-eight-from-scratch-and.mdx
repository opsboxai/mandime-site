RUF just dropped a 1,000-horsepower, twin-turbo flat-eight engine that's straight out of a dream. This isn't some slapped-together project; it's a clean-sheet 4.8-liter beast with zero hybrid assistance, just pure displacement and boost. And get this, it's paired with a six-speed manual transmission, because RUF knows that a thousand horsepower and three pedals are a match made in heaven.

The B8 prototype, dubbed the "Erprober," is a CTR3 stretched to accommodate the wider engine. The matte black body with Blossom Yellow accents and figure-eight graphics pays homage to RUF's iconic Yellowbird. This isn't just about horsepower; it's about history and the future of automotive engineering. RUF is carving out a new chapter with a production road car that's never been done before: a flat-eight in a road car.

If you're into the raw, unfiltered power of a naturally aspirated engine with a manual transmission, this is your dream come true. The B8 isn't just a testbed; it's a statement that RUF is still pushing the boundaries of what's possible. Catch it in action at the Goodwood Festival of Speed, where it's set to make a splash.
