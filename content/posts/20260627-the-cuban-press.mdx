Chris Bumstead, aka "Lion Mind," breaks down the Cuban press in this video, and it's a move that could seriously upgrade your chest workout. The Cuban press isn't just about lifting weights; it's about building strength and stability in a way that traditional bench presses can't match. Bumstead starts with the setup, emphasizing the importance of a solid base. You start with the barbell on the floor, grab it with a close grip, and then press it up to your chest, all while maintaining control and balance.

The key to the Cuban press is the transition from the floor to the chest. Bumstead demonstrates how to keep your core tight and your feet planted to ensure you're not using momentum to lift the weight. This move requires serious core strength and stability, making it a great addition to any routine for those looking to build a strong, functional upper body. The video also highlights the importance of proper form, which can prevent injuries and maximize gains.

Bumstead walks you through the technique step-by-step, showing how to lower the barbell to the floor and then press it back up to the chest, all while maintaining control. He also shares some tips on how to progress the move, suggesting that beginners start with a lighter weight and focus on mastering the form before adding more resistance.

For guys who are tired of the same old bench press routine, the Cuban press offers a fresh challenge. It's not just about lifting heavy; it's about building a strong, stable upper body that can handle real-world challenges. So, if you're looking to spice up your chest workout and build some serious strength, give the Cuban press a try. Just make sure you've got your form down first.
