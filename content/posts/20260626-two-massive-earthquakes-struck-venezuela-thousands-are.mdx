Two massive earthquakes struck Venezuela less than a minute apart on Wednesday, shaking the ground across the country, including the capital Caracas. The second quake, measuring 7.5 on the Richter scale, is the strongest to hit the country in over 125 years. 

The interim president, Delcy Rodríguez, declared a state of emergency and confirmed that at least 164 people have died, with over 971 injured. The United States Geological Survey warns that the final death toll could exceed 10,000, based on modeling and previous earthquakes. More than 10,000 people have been reported missing by their families on a dedicated website.

Rodríguez addressed the nation, urging citizens to stay united and asking all healthcare workers to report to their workplaces to assist the injured. The country's largest airport in Maiquetía was closed due to serious damage, and many people spent the night on the streets, fearing aftershocks.

The United States has pledged immediate support, with Secretary of State Marco Rubio announcing the deployment of search and rescue teams, medical resources, and humanitarian assistance. President Donald Trump also promised support in a Truth Social post, emphasizing the need to help the affected population.
