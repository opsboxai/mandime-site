Flipper Devices, the company behind the controversial Flipper Zero hacking tool, is now offering a gadget to help you get some peace and quiet. Introducing the Busy Bar, a $249 hardware clock with an LED screen that doubles as a "BUSY" sign to keep the interruptions at bay. 

Callum Tennent, a creative writer at Flipper, explains that the Busy Bar is designed to give you a polite yet firm way to signal that you're busy. The device can display messages like "BUSY" or even "GO AWAY" to deter those pesky interruptions. It's essentially a high-tech "On Air" light, but with a twist. The Busy Bar can also integrate with smart home devices, turning your lights red to broadcast your busy status to the entire house.

The Busy Bar isn't just a fancy distraction blocker; it's a hardware solution to a problem that software tools often can't solve. Tennent points out the irony of needing to pick up your phone to stop yourself from picking it up. The Busy Bar offers manual controls, including a big button to start or pause your focus sessions, and it can even sync with your phone via Wi-Fi. 

While some might call it over-engineered, Tennent argues that once you get your hands on it, the device's capabilities become clear. The Busy Bar is open-source and can be easily disassembled, making it a robust and customizable tool for those who want to reclaim their focus. 

Flipper Devices may have made a name for themselves with their hacking tools, but with the Busy Bar, they're offering a gadget that could finally give you the peace of mind you need to get things done.
