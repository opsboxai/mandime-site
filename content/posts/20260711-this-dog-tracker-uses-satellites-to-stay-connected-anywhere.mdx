Fi Ultra, the latest dog tracker from Fi, is a game-changer for those who live off the beaten path. This device uses T-Satellite with Starlink, LTE, and always-on GPS to keep tabs on your furry friend, even in the most remote areas. The big deal here is the satellite connectivity, which ensures you can track your dog nearly anywhere in the US, even where cell service is non-existent.

I’ve been testing the Ultra on my own pup, Charlie, and the speed of tracking updates has been impressive. In the wild, a few seconds can make a huge difference. The Ultra keeps up with Charlie's movements in real-time, which is crucial for those moments when she decides to take off. Battery life has also been solid, lasting about two days, which is a fair trade-off for the always-on GPS and satellite capabilities.

The clip attachment, which I was skeptical about, has held up surprisingly well. Charlie hasn’t managed to knock it loose, even during her most rambunctious moments. The Ultra also includes features like Safe Zone alerts and a sound-and-vibration callback, which can be a lifesaver when you need to get your dog's attention.

However, the cost is a significant hurdle. At $199 for the hardware and a $189 annual subscription, it's not a cheap investment. Plus, while the Ultra can track your dog via satellite, you still need a data connection on your phone to view updates in the Fi app. This means that in truly remote areas, you might not be able to see real-time updates, which is a notable limitation.

Overall, the Fi Ultra is a solid choice for rural dog owners and outdoor enthusiasts who need reliable tracking in areas with poor cell service. Just be prepared to shell out for the hardware and subscription, and remember that you’ll still need a data connection to see those updates.
