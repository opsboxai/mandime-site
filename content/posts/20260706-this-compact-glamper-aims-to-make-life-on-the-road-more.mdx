Winnebago's Elora, also known as the Resa, is a game-changer for those who dream of hitting the road but can't quite commit to a full-on RV. This compact rig, based on the Ram ProMaster chassis, is a Class C setup that measures just 20 feet long, making it a lot more manageable than your average motorhome.

The Elora strikes a perfect balance between a van and an RV. It offers a surprising amount of interior space, with a ceiling height of 6.5 feet and a width of 6.75 feet. This translates to a more livable space, complete with a full shower, cassette toilet, galley, refrigerator, and ample storage. The rear interior garage is modular, capable of carrying up to 2,200 pounds of payload, and can be converted into a lounge or a power lift bed for queen and elevated double bunk sleeping.

What's particularly impressive is that despite its compact size, the Elora doesn't skimp on features. It comes equipped with dual 30-gallon fresh and grey water tanks, chassis-fueled heating, and a 5kWh battery to power onboard electronics. The interior is well-appointed with dimmable LED lighting, charging ports, and even Starlink prep. And for those looking to take it off the grid, Winnebago offers options like solar panels and hybrid generators.

At a starting price of $153,772, the Elora is a lot more affordable than some of the ultra-luxe RVs that can cost a quarter-million or more. This makes it a practical and appealing option for those who want to enjoy the freedom of the open road without breaking the bank. Whether you're a seasoned camper or a first-timer, the Elora offers a perfect blend of comfort and affordability.
