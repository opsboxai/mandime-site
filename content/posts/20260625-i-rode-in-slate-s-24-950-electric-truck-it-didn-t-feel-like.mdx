Slate's $24,950 electric truck is a curious blend of affordability and customization, but it's not without its caveats. Sara Lacey recently took a spin in the vehicle, and her insights reveal a truck that feels more premium than its price tag suggests.

When Slate first announced their electric truck, it was touted as a budget-friendly option with a starting price in the low $20,000s. However, with federal incentives now out of the picture, the base model now sits at $24,950. Despite this, Slate has seen a surge of interest, with 10,000 pre-orders coming in on the first day. But the real question is whether this price point still offers value.

The Slate Truck's appeal lies in its customization options. A fully loaded model with the SUV kit, lift, and accessories can top out around $35,000. This flexibility allows buyers to tailor the truck to their needs, but it also means the final price can quickly climb. 

One of the standout features is the truck's DIY approach. You can order the SUV kit and install it yourself, or outsource the work to a local mechanic. The truck's design also caters to those who want to wrap it, with a boxy shape that makes it easy to customize. 

Lacey's test drive revealed a surprisingly comfortable ride. The bucket seats are soft and wide, and the legroom is generous, even in the back seat of the SUV trim. The roll-up windows are sturdy, and the dash has a shelf that extends across the entire truck, with space for a tablet or smartphone.

The truck's simplicity is both a strength and a potential drawback. It operates as a one-pedal driver with no adjustable regenerative braking, which keeps things straightforward but might not appeal to those looking for more advanced features. 

While the Slate Truck may not feel like a $24,950 vehicle, it's important to consider the overall cost of ownership. EVs generally require less maintenance, and the absence of gas costs can be a significant saving. However, the lack of federal incentives means buyers need to weigh the initial price against long-term savings.

In the end, the Slate Truck offers a unique blend of affordability and customization, but it's up to the buyer to decide if the value proposition still holds. Whether you're looking for a simple, customizable ride or a more premium experience, the Slate Truck is worth considering.
