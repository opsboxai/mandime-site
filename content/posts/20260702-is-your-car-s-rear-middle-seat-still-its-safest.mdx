It's been a long-standing belief that the middle rear seat is the safest spot in a car. But guess what? Things have changed. According to a 2017 NHTSA study, back in the '60s and '70s, the rear seats were significantly safer than the front. Sitting in the middle of the second row was 37% safer than the front seats. But here's the kicker: that huge safety gap has nearly vanished.

Modern cars are now safer than ever, thanks to improved structural integrity and advanced safety tech. Front passengers are also far more likely to buckle up, which has dramatically reduced fatalities. In fact, using a three-point belt in the middle back seat cuts your chance of death by 58% in sedans and 75% in SUVs. But with more airbags and better seat belt tech in the front, the safety advantage of the rear middle seat has become much less pronounced.

However, there's one group that still benefits immensely from sitting in the middle back seat: children. Properly securing kids in the back with specialized seats can make a huge difference. So, while the rear middle seat might not be as much of a safety haven as it once was, it's still crucial to buckle up, especially when you've got little ones on board. Because at the end of the day, safety isn't just about where you sit; it's about making sure you're strapped in tight.
