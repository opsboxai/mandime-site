McLaren is allegedly cooking up a hypercar called the P50 with a manual transmission, and that alone should tell you everything about where the industry's head is right now. The hypercar space has gotten so aggressively automated that a company even considering a stick shift feels like actual news. Ferrari just dropped the 12Cilindri with a manual option, Koenigsegg did the same with the CC850, and now McLaren wants in on what's becoming a weird flex of engineering commitment. The question nobody's asking out loud yet: is it a real three-pedal manual or a hydraulic simulation dressed up to look like one? Ferrari and Koenigsegg both went the route of electronic fakes that feel close enough, which is the smart move when you're hitting triple-digit speeds and your driver might be older than your brake discs. But here's what matters more than the tech. These guys are betting that the people dropping eight figures on a hypercar actually want to feel like they're driving it, not just steering a computer. That's either the most cynical marketing play in recent memory or a genuine admission that the full-autonomous shift happened too fast and nobody's satisfied anymore. Either way, if McLaren pulls the trigger, we'll find out which camp they're in.
