It's a car lover's dream come true: nine prototypes that were thought to be lost forever have resurfaced. Prototypes are often destroyed to keep designs under wraps, but these survivors are a rare find. 

One of the most notable is the 1966 Dodge Charger R/T prototype, which disappeared after a crash test in 1965. It resurfaced in 2016, with its original body panels and VIN intact. Another is the 1968 Chevrolet Camaro Z/28, which was found in a barn in 2014, still in its original condition.

These cars aren't just relics; they're a window into automotive history. Each one represents a moment in time when manufacturers were pushing the boundaries of design and engineering. The fact that they've been found is a testament to the enduring fascination with these early concepts.

For car enthusiasts, these prototypes are more than just vehicles; they're pieces of automotive art. The discovery of these cars offers a glimpse into what could have been and what was nearly lost to time. It's a reminder that sometimes, the most valuable things are the ones we thought we'd never see again.
