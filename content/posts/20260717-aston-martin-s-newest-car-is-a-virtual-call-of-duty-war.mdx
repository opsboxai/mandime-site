Aston Martin just designed a tank for a video game. Not a real tank. A fictional one called the Dreadnought that's dropping in Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 4, and it looks exactly like what you'd get if someone told an actual luxury car designer to stop worrying about physics or road laws or, really, anything except making something that looks absolutely lethal.

The company didn't phone this in either. They built a full digital render that ties back to Aston Martin's actual design language—the grille, the proportions, the obsessive attention to detail that they'd normally waste on a 300k supercar. Except this thing has armor plating and what appears to be a turret. It's the automotive equivalent of a concept car, except instead of previewing next year's lineup, it's previewing next year's kill streak.

What's wild here isn't that a luxury brand partnered with a game. That's standard. It's that someone at Aston Martin saw this brief and thought, yeah, we'll do our actual design work on this. They didn't slap a logo on an existing vehicle model. They made something new. The Dreadnought sits in this weird space where it's fake enough that nobody's buying it at a dealership, but real enough as an idea that you can see how a designer working for a 110-year-old British carmaker got genuinely interested in the problem. What does an Aston Martin look like when it's not bound by anything except looking cool and making sense in a warzone?
