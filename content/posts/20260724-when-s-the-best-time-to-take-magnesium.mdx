Most guys pop magnesium right before bed because they heard it helps you sleep, but timing matters way more than that if you actually want the stuff to work. The real answer depends on what you're taking it for, and there's enough variance in how your body processes it that getting this wrong basically tanks the benefit.

Magnesium plays a role in muscle recovery, sleep quality, and stress management, but absorption is the bottleneck. Taking it with food slows absorption, which sounds bad until you realize that slower absorption means it stays in your system longer and actually gives your body time to use it instead of just flushing it out. On an empty stomach it hits faster but also leaves faster. If you're taking it for sleep, the conventional wisdom of taking it an hour or two before bed actually makes sense because you want peak levels when you're trying to wind down. But if you're taking it for training recovery or just general magnesium status, splitting your dose across the day with meals gets you more consistent levels throughout the 24 hours.

The other thing nobody mentions is form matters. Magnesium glycinate absorbs differently than magnesium oxide, which is why some people swear by one and get nothing from the other. And the gastrointestinal side effects everyone warns you about mostly happen when you overload on certain forms or take it all at once on an empty stomach. Start with 200 to 400 milligrams a day, time it around your actual goal (sleep, recovery, stress), and give it at least two weeks before you decide whether it's doing anything. Patience works better than guessing.
