Courtney Wheeler, the costume designer behind The Bear, has been a menswear game-changer. From Carmy’s loopwheel tees to Fak’s vintage shirts, every piece of clothing on the show has been meticulously curated. But it's the white tees that really made waves. Carmy's daily uniform became a cult favorite, with fans scrambling to find the exact same loopwheel tees from brands like Merz B. Schwanen and Whitesville. These tees aren't just about style; they're about quality and practicality. As Wheeler puts it, "He’s pretty much wearing the same things every day, but he has a good eye and good taste."

The NN.07 Gael jacket is another standout. Initially, showrunner Chris Storer wanted Carmy to wear a peacoat, but after a failed search, Wheeler found the perfect jacket at Bloomingdale’s. The Gael jacket became Carmy's go-to winter staple, and it quickly became a must-have for fans. Its practicality and versatility make it a standout piece that can be worn every day without feeling like cosplay.

Fak’s vintage tees are another highlight. Wheeler and her team sourced half true vintage and half new tees that were aged down to perfection. The result? Tees that look like they’ve lived a life before hitting the screen. Wheeler’s favorite? The fruit bowl tee from the series finale. 

The Bear's costume design isn't just about aesthetics; it's about telling a story through clothing. From Carmy’s timeless white tees to Fak’s vintage shirts, every piece adds a layer of depth to the characters. As the show comes to a close, Wheeler's work has not only elevated the style of The Bear but also influenced the menswear world in a significant way.
