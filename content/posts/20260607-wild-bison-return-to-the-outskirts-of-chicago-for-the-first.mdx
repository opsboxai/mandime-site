Sixty miles from the heart of Chicago, wild bison are grazing on the prairie for the first time in over 200 years. Kathleen Rellihan reports that these iconic animals have returned to the Burlington Prairie, a stunning comeback for a species that once roamed freely across the Midwest. This isn't just a conservation win; it's a glimpse into a past where bison were a cornerstone of the American landscape. The bison's return marks a significant step in restoring the natural balance of the region, bringing a piece of history back to life.
