If you’re after six-pack abs, you’re in the right place. Dean Stattmann breaks down four core workouts that will rip your midsection to shreds, giving you a set of abs that could double as a cheese grater. But these exercises aren’t just about aesthetics. A strong core is the driving force behind almost every athletic movement, and it’s one of your best defenses against injury and back pain.

First up, let’s talk about the often-overlooked lower back muscles. These aren’t just about looking good; they’re crucial for stability and injury prevention. The lumbar extension on a Roman chair is a killer move for hitting these muscles. According to Luke Carlson, founder and CEO of Discover Strength, this exercise is more favorable than back extension machines because it focuses on lumbar extension without involving the glutes. Start with your body in a straight line from your feet to your head, and slowly lower your upper body until your torso goes just past parallel with the floor. Pause, then engage your lower back to return to the starting position. For added resistance, hold a weight plate against your chest, but start with just your body weight if you’re new to the exercise.

Next, it’s time to hit your obliques, the sides of your core. The Paloff press is a simple yet highly effective anti-rotational exercise that can be done with either a cable or a band. This move helps stabilize against rotational forces, which is great for sports like golf or tennis. You can perform it from either a standing or kneeling position, depending on your preference and the sport you’re training for.

Moving on, rotational exercises are key for power development and creating control of rotation. The torso rotation machine is an accessible option that targets both the internal and external obliques. Look for the machine that resembles a lateral raise but with a pad for your knees. The goal is to twist as far as possible without pulling with your arms. Let your midsection do all the work, those extra degrees won’t benefit your core or your arms.

Finally, it’s time to hit those six-pack muscles, the rectus abdominis. Abdominal flexion exercises are your go-to for targeting this muscle head-on. These moves will give you the definition you’re after, but remember, building muscle isn’t the same as burning fat. Even the sharpest abs can’t cut through a spare tire. So, while these workouts will help you build a stronger, more defined core, you’ll still need to focus on overall fat loss to see those abs peek through.

Whether you’re obsessed with getting abs by summer or couldn’t care less about what goes on under your shirt, these core workouts deserve a spot in every guy’s workout rotation. So, hit the gym, and let’s get those abs shredded.
