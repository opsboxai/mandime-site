Chris Bumstead, aka "The Caveman," just dropped a video that's going to make you rethink your press setup. In it, he breaks down the Czech Press, a unique exercise that's been gaining traction among strength athletes. The Caveman isn't one to shy away from unconventional methods, and this move is no exception.

The Czech Press, also known as the Deficit Overhead Press, involves standing on a platform or bench to create a deficit, which forces you to start the press from a lower position. This adds a new dimension to the overhead press, engaging your core and improving your lockout strength. The Caveman demonstrates the move with precision, showing how to maintain proper form and avoid common pitfalls.

What makes this video particularly compelling is The Caveman's detailed explanation of the biomechanics involved. He breaks down how the deficit affects your range of motion and how it can help you build a stronger, more stable shoulder girdle. The Caveman also shares his personal experience with the exercise, noting how it's helped him push through plateaus in his overhead pressing.
