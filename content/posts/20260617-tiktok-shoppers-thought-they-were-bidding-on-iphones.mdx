TikTok shoppers thought they were bidding on iPhones, but got teddy bears instead. Sellers used high-end items to draw in bidders to "Surprise Sets" livestreams, only to end up with cheap prizes. Gambling experts are worried about the potential for harm, and it's a clever, if sketchy, marketing tactic. These Surprise Sets are essentially a digital version of a carnival game, where the real prize is the seller's profit. It's a reminder to always read the fine print, or in this case, the small print about what you're actually bidding on.
