The Honda CR-V has snagged the top spot in U.S. car sales for the first half of 2026, a position typically held by the Ford F-150 or Toyota RAV4. With 226,114 units sold, the CR-V's rise to the top is a testament to its enduring popularity and the supply chain woes of its competitors. 

The F-150 has been hit hard by aluminum shortages, thanks to a fire at a Novelis-owned plant in 2025, leading to a production shortfall of 17,600 units. Meanwhile, Toyota's RAV4 is dealing with production bottlenecks as it transitions to its sixth generation. These supply constraints have given the CR-V a rare opportunity to shine, bolstered by its hybrid trims and the TrailSport variant.

While the CR-V's reign might be short-lived, it's a reminder of how supply chain issues can shake up the automotive market. With the F-150 and RAV4 production issues easing, the race for the top spot is far from over. But for now, the Honda CR-V is the king of the hill.
