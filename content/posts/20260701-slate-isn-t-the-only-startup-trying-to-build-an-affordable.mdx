REO Industries is betting big on a new pickup truck that could shake up the market. The company, with roots tracing back to Ransom E. Olds (yes, the same Olds from Oldsmobile), is reviving a name that once defined American innovation. The REO Runabout is set to start at just $21,500, making it a serious contender in the affordable pickup segment.

But here’s the kicker: REO Industries is sticking with good ol’ gas power, offering a proven four-cylinder engine that promises at least 25 miles per gallon and up to 600 miles on a single tank. That’s a solid range for a truck that can also tow 4,500 pounds. The company is emphasizing reliability and direct-to-consumer sales, which could be a game-changer in an industry dominated by legacy automakers.

REO Industries is clearly aiming to capture the nostalgia of the original REO Motor Car Company while delivering a modern, affordable pickup. If they can pull it off, they might just give the big players a run for their money. The company is currently taking $25 refundable deposits, with plans to unveil the full lineup by 2027 and start production in 2028. Stay tuned, because this could be the pickup truck that finally makes sense for your wallet and your garage.
