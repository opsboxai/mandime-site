The Ferrari Testarossa, Lamborghini Countach, and Porsche 944 Turbo were the stuff of dreams in the '80s. These cars were the epitome of speed and style, but now they're getting smoked by a Skoda Elroq vRS, a crossover that's about as exciting as a paperclip. 

At Dunsfold track, the Elroq vRS, with its 335 horsepower and all-wheel drive, walked away from these '80s legends. The Testarossa, with its iconic looks and V12 engine, couldn't keep up. The Countach, with its scissor doors and raw power, was left in the dust. And the 944 Turbo, with its turbocharged flat-six, couldn't match the Skoda's launch control and torque vectoring.

It's not just about raw power; it's about how you access it. The Elroq's electric drivetrain and advanced launch control give it an edge over the manual transmissions and turbo lag of the '80s cars. But let's be real, the '80s supercars still have the emotional appeal. They're the ones you'd want to see on your bedroom wall, not a crossover. 

While the Elroq vRS is practical and efficient, with a range of up to 344 miles, it's hard to deny the nostalgia and raw excitement of those classic supercars. The '80s may have been the golden age of supercars, but today's tech has made them obsolete in a straight-line race.
