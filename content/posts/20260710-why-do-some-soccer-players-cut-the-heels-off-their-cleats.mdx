Pedro Neto, the Portuguese forward, made headlines at the World Cup when he was spotted playing with the heels of his cleats cut off. This isn't just a quirky fashion choice; it's a practical solution to a common problem in soccer: heel discomfort. 

The modification isn't new; it's a longstanding practice among players who deal with everything from blisters to more serious conditions like Haglund’s syndrome. This condition, characterized by a bony growth on the back of the heel, can cause significant pain and inflammation, especially when wearing tight-fitting cleats. 

For Neto and others, cutting off the heel of the cleat is a DIY solution to a painful problem. It's a loophole in the regulations, similar to the way players customize their sock lengths. The cleats in question, Nike Mercurial Vapor 16 Elite, are modified to fit the unique needs of each player, ensuring they can perform at their best without the distraction of discomfort. 

While the image of Neto's cleats went viral, it's a reminder of the often-overlooked details that can make or break a player's performance. In a sport where every millimeter matters, finding a way to stay comfortable is key.
