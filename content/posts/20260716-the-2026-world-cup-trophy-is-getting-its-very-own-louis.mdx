The 2026 World Cup trophy is getting a Louis Vuitton trunk, which is the kind of absurd luxury flex that somehow makes perfect sense. The fashion house designed a custom case specifically to transport the golden trophy during the tournament in North America, and yeah, it's exactly what you'd expect: hand-crafted, monogrammed, probably costs more than a car.

Here's where it gets interesting though. Louis Vuitton has been the official trunk maker for the World Cup since 1998, so this isn't some random sponsorship deal slapped together last month. They've built cases for every tournament for nearly 30 years. The trunk for 2026 is modular, designed to be opened and displayed on stage during ceremonies, which means it's not just luggage—it's part of the show now. Every time they reveal the trophy, you're looking at a piece of designed equipment that's meant to be as iconic as the moment itself.

This is actually how trophy culture works at the highest level. The physical object needs protection, but it also needs theater. A wooden box doesn't cut it when you're moving a symbol worth global broadcast rights worth billions. Louis Vuitton's angle here is clear: they're not selling trunks to you. They're wrapping themselves around one of the few moments on earth where a billion people are watching the same thing simultaneously. That's brand placement that money can't technically buy, because you can only get it if you've been trusted with it for three decades straight.
