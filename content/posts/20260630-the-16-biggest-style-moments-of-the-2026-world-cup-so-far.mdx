Erling Haaland's Hermès Birkin 50 is the ultimate flex at the 2026 World Cup. The Norwegian striker's bag, adorned with the "Endless Road" edition's colorful artwork, is a mid-five-figure statement that screams luxury. But it's not just Haaland who's making waves with his style. DR Congo's team arrived in advanced tailoring, complete with bedazzled leopard brooches and animal-print harnesses, making them one of the best-dressed squads at the tournament.

Japan's retro-inspired Adidas tracksuits are another standout. The color-blocking and boxy silhouette give them a vintage vibe, while the throwback JFA crest adds a touch of old-world heraldry. Meanwhile, Bukayo Saka's New Balance Furon Elite FG V9s, reworked with Stone Island's heat-reactive technology, shift color as he gets more involved in the game, adding a futuristic touch to his kit.

And let's not forget the social media sensation: Future performing "Like That" in a custom Louis Vuitton jacket. It's the kind of unexpected crossover that only the World Cup can produce. From the Scottish fans in kilts to the pink boots that are impossible to miss, the 2026 World Cup is proving to be a style spectacle. So, whether you're on the pitch or in the stands, there's no shortage of fashion moments to admire.
