A bison in Yellowstone National Park launched a 65-year-old man 8 feet into the air. The scariest part? He was doing everything right. The man, who was walking with his grandson, kept a safe distance from the bison, but the animal still attacked. Photographer Mike MacLeod, who witnessed the incident, noted that the bison initially charged a group of kids and a pickup truck before targeting the man. 

This attack highlights the unpredictable nature of wildlife, especially during mating season. Bison are the largest land mammals in North America, with males weighing up to 2,000 pounds. Despite following National Park Service guidelines to stay at least 25 yards away from large animals, the man was still injured. The incident serves as a stark reminder that wild animals are just that, wild. 

During the bison rut, which runs from June to September, testosterone levels spike, making these usually docile grazers extremely aggressive. Even if you're doing everything right, bison can perceive you as a threat. Drimal from the Greater Yellowstone Coalition advises paying attention to warning signs like head bobbing, raised tails, and bellowing. If you notice any of these behaviors, back away immediately. 

The takeaway? Yellowstone is a unique place to experience wildlife, but it's crucial to respect the animals' space and stay alert. Even when you're following the rules, wild animals can still be dangerous.
