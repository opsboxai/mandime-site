Will Tennyson breaks down the fast food habits of America's fattest cities, and it's not pretty. The video dives into how fast food consumption correlates with obesity rates, revealing some eye-opening stats. For instance, in McAllen, Texas, the city with the highest obesity rate, residents consume an average of 120 fast food meals per year. That's about two meals a week. 

Tennyson also highlights how these cities often lack access to healthier food options, pushing residents toward the convenience of fast food joints. The video isn't just about numbers; it's a stark reminder of how our food choices can impact our health. If you're trying to stay fit and healthy, this might make you think twice about hitting the drive-thru.
