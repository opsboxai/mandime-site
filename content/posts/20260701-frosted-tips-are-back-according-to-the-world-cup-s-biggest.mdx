Frosted tips are making a major comeback, and the 2026 World Cup is ground zero for this Y2K revival. From Enner Valencia's bleached close-crop to Tahith Chong's dip-dyed curls, players are using their hair to make a statement on and off the field. 

It's not just about standing out in a sea of jerseys; frosted tips have become a key part of an athlete's personal branding. Just ask David Beckham, who famously sported a blond halo. Today, the trend is more subtle, with finer, diffused highlights that complement a player's natural hair texture. 

What's wild is how versatile this look has become. It works on straight hair, curls, and coils, giving it a modern, global feel. So whether you're cheering on your team or just looking for a fresh hair idea, the frosted tips trend is worth a second look.
