Angela Nikolau and Ivan Beerkus, the daredevils who starred in the 2024 Netflix documentary "Skywalkers: A Love Story," are now in police custody after their high-altitude marriage proposal atop the Empire State Building. The couple, known for their extreme climbs, scaled the 1,454-foot skyscraper and unfurled a banner with a Jimi Hendrix quote, drawing attention and immediate police response.

This isn't the first time Nikolau and Beerkus have pushed the boundaries of what's legal and safe. In the documentary, Nikolau, a former gymnast and daughter of circus performers, positions herself as a revolutionary figure in a male-dominated field. The duo's climbs are not just about reaching the top; they're about breaking rules and defying limits. They describe their methods of accessing tall structures, from picking locks to dressing up as construction workers to blend in.

But their latest stunt has landed them in hot water. New York law strictly prohibits climbing structures over 50 feet without permission. The NYPD responded swiftly, with highly trained officers scaling the spire to apprehend the couple. The Empire State Building, while welcoming legal marriage proposals on its observation deck, clearly wasn't thrilled with this unauthorized climb.

For Nikolau and Beerkus, the thrill of the climb and the rush of breaking rules are part of their allure. But as they face legal consequences, it's a reminder that there are real-world repercussions for their actions. The couple's story is a testament to the thin line between daring feats and criminal behavior.
