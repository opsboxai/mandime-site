Temperatures may be soaring, but the chill on streaming services this month is unmissable. Director Yorgos Lanthimos's twisted take on Frankenstein, *Poor Things*, is now on Netflix, and it's a surreal, darkly comedic ride. But if you're in the mood for something more haunting, Netflix also has *I Am Frankelda*, a mesmerizing stop-motion animated film that's a darkly captivating mix of *The Nightmare Before Christmas*, *Pan’s Labyrinth*, and *Alice in Wonderland*.

In *I Am Frankelda*, Francisca Imelda (voiced by Mireya Mendoza and Claudis Bridgeforth) is an aspiring young author in late 1800s Mexico with a penchant for the fantastic and macabre. She's whisked away to the monstrous world of Topus Terrentus, where she's tasked with becoming the realm’s new “nightmare teller.” The film is a visual masterpiece, with stunning animation that brings the eerie world to life. It's a must-watch for fans of the supernatural and stop-motion animation.

For those who prefer their chills with a side of laughs, *Poor Things* is a reimagining of Frankenstein that's as dark as it is funny. Directed by Yorgos Lanthimos, the film follows mad scientist Godwin Baxter (Willem Dafoe) and his “daughter” Bella (Emma Stone), a reanimated dead woman with a childlike disposition. The story is a surreal and darkly comedic exploration of life, death, and everything in between. It's a visually lavish, almost indescribable strange experience that's perfect for a summer night.

If you're in the mood for some classic cinema, Hulu has reinstated Steven Spielberg's *A.I. Artificial Intelligence*, which feels eerily prescient in 2026. The film explores the emotional journey of David (Haley Joel Osment), a humanoid robot in a world on the brink of collapse. Spielberg's blend of family drama and sci-fi raises interesting questions about what it means to be human, making it a powerful watch.

For a dose of nostalgia, Prime Video has brought back all three *Bill & Ted* films. From the original *Bill & Ted’s Excellent Adventure* to the 2020 sequel *Bill & Ted Face the Music*, the trilogy is a perfect blend of time-traveling comedy and the unifying power of rock 'n' roll. It's a reminder that being excellent to each other is always in style.

And if you're a fan of James Bond, Criterion Channel is declassifying Sean Connery's first outings as 007, with *Dr. No*, *From Russia With Love*, and *Goldfinger* now available to stream. These films are foundational to the Bond franchise and a must-watch for any fan of the genre.

Whether you're in the mood for supernatural thrills, dark comedy, or classic cinema, there's something for everyone streaming this month. So grab some popcorn, settle in, and enjoy the ride.
