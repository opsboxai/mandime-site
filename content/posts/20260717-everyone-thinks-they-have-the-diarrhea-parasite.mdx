If you’ve been scrolling through your social feeds lately, you might feel like everyone you know is dealing with explosive diarrhea. Welcome to the world of cyclosporiasis. Joye Pate, a 28-year-old who recently returned from New York, found herself in a bathroom cycle that lasted a full week. Her symptoms, hourly diarrhea, stomach cramps, and a general sense of being unwell, fit the bill for cyclospora, a parasitic infection that’s been spreading across the US.

But here’s the kicker: Pate never tested positive for cyclospora. Yet, her experience is a microcosm of what’s happening across the country. The CDC reports nearly 7,000 cases, but experts believe the actual number is much higher. Social media is amplifying the anxiety, with countless posts and TikToks about the parasite, making it feel like it’s everywhere.

The irony is that cyclospora isn’t as common as other foodborne pathogens, and routine stool tests don’t typically screen for it. This bottleneck in diagnosis means many people with symptoms might never know if they have it. And with symptoms like explosive diarrhea and stomach cramps, it’s easy to jump to conclusions.

For those who are worried, the best course of action is to call your doctor and ask for a stool test. Cyclospora is treatable with antibiotics, but prevention is key. Wash your fruits and veggies thoroughly, and be mindful of where you’re eating. The parasite is often linked to leafy greens and salads, so it’s worth being cautious.

In the end, it’s a balancing act between staying informed and not letting anxiety take over. As Colin Carlson, an epidemiologist, put it, “Do not get the diarrhea parasite.” But if you do, know that it’s treatable, and you’re not alone in your bathroom battles.
