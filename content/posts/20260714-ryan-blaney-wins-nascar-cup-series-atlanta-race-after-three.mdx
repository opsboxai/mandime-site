Ryan Blaney's win at the NASCAR Cup Series race in Atlanta was a testament to his resilience and strategy. After sweeping both stages and leading 171 of 260 laps, Blaney had to endure a three-hour rain delay that turned the race into a marathon. During the delay, Blaney did what any sensible driver would do: he took a nap. 

When the race resumed, Blaney was fresh and ready to battle it out with Bubba Wallace. Despite hitting the wall and dealing with a vibration, Blaney managed to stay in the lead. The race's final moments were chaotic, with overtime and penalties adding to the drama. Wallace's controversial move below the yellow line cost him a top-three finish, and he was sent to the back of the lead lap.

Blaney's win is his second of the season, solidifying his position as a main challenger to Tyler Reddick and Denny Hamlin. The race showcased not just Blaney's skill but also his ability to adapt to unpredictable conditions. In a sport where every second counts, Blaney's strategic nap and his ability to push through adversity are worth noting. This win is a reminder that in NASCAR, sometimes the best strategy is to catch a few Zs.
