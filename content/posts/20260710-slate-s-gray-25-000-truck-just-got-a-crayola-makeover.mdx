Slate Auto, backed by Jeff Bezos, just teamed up with Crayola to bring some much-needed color to its gray $25,000 electric truck. The five vibrant wraps, Cerulean, Fern, Jersey Tomato, Razzmatazz, and Dandelion, are straight out of a crayon box, and WIRED's pick is the hot-pink Razzmatazz. 

The idea is simple: Slate's truck is designed to be wrapped, not painted, to keep costs down. The Crayola starter packs come with decals, a color-coordinated key-fob cap, and a dashboard accessory called a Slatelet. But here's the catch: these colorful transformations aren't exactly budget-friendly. A Crayola starter pack will set you back $1,550, which is three times the cost of a standard wrap. 

While Slate's pitch is about affordable customization, the Crayola packs might feel like a step back. The truck itself is a no-frills, gray composite body with manual windows and no touchscreen, all to hit that $24,950 price point. The wraps have been part of the plan from the start, with over 100 hues available, but the Crayola edition is a bit of a splurge. 

Still, if you're looking to make a statement on the road, a Jersey Tomato or Razzmatazz Slate might just be worth the extra cash. Delivery is expected to start in the fourth quarter of 2026, with most shipments going out in 2027. So, if you're ready to brighten up your ride, it's time to preorder and pick your crayon color.
