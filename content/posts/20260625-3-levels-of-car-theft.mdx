Mark Rober breaks down the three levels of car theft, and it's wild. The first level is the classic smash-and-grab, where thieves break a window and steal your stuff. But it gets way more interesting from there.

Level two involves a bit of tech savvy. Thieves use a device called a "code grabber" to intercept the signal from your key fob when you lock your car. They then use that signal to unlock and start your car. Rober shows how easy it is to get one of these devices online, and it's alarming how many people are falling victim to this method.

But it's level three that's truly mind-blowing. Rober demonstrates how thieves can use a "relay box" to trick your car into thinking your key fob is nearby, even if it's miles away. This allows them to unlock and drive off with your car without ever touching it. The setup is surprisingly simple, and it's a stark reminder of how vulnerable modern cars can be.

Rober also shares some tips to protect yourself. For starters, keep your key fob in a Faraday bag or a metal box when you're not using it to block the signal. And always park in well-lit areas or in a garage if possible. It's a sobering watch, but it's worth knowing how to stay one step ahead of these tech-savvy thieves.
