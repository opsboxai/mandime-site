Dominic Sessa, the 23-year-old actor who plays a young Anthony Bourdain in the upcoming biopic Tony, is already making waves. The guy has the perfect mix of scruff and charisma to pull off the role, and he's got the chops to boot. Sessa's journey from high school drama class to Hollywood stardom is a wild ride, and his portrayal of a young Bourdain is just the latest stop on that journey.

Sessa's breakout role in Alexander Payne's The Holdovers put him on the map, but he's not resting on his laurels. He's taken on smaller parts in a variety of films, learning from the best without being the face of every project. Now, with Tony, he's back in the spotlight, playing one of the most beloved figures of the 21st century. The film isn't just about Bourdain's culinary journey; it's about his formative years, when he was a "spoiled, miserable, narcissistic, self-destructive and thoughtless young lout." Sessa nails that transition, capturing Bourdain's charm and exasperation with ease.

But Sessa isn't just a young actor in a biopic. He's a real person with his own story. Growing up in southern New Jersey, he channeled his performative streak into dance and ice hockey. After his dad's sudden death at 46, Sessa found solace in acting, eventually landing the role that would change his life. Now, as he navigates his next chapter, Sessa is proving that he's more than just a pretty face, he's a talent to watch.
