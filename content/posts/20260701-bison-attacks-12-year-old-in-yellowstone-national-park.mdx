A bison attack in Yellowstone National Park isn’t exactly rare, but when it involves a 12-year-old kid, it’s a whole different story. The National Park Service (NPS) reported that the incident happened near Mud Volcano, just north of Fishing Bridge, and the kid was rushed to a nearby hospital. The NPS is still investigating, and details are sparse, but it’s a stark reminder of the danger lurking in one of America’s most beloved parks.

Yellowstone is known for its stunning landscapes and wildlife, but it’s also a place where bison can turn aggressive in a heartbeat. These massive beasts have injured more people than any other animal in the park. In 2015, the worst year on record, five people were injured by bison. On average, there’s only one or two incidents a year, but that’s still too many. Between 1978 and 1992, 56 visitors were injured by bison, and two were killed. From 2000 to 2015, 25 more were injured.

The NPS warns visitors to stay at least 25 yards away from bison, elk, and moose, and 100 yards away from bears, wolves, and cougars. But it’s clear that not everyone heeds these warnings. The Interior Department has even directed park officials to be more selective about notifying the public about serious incidents, which might make you wonder how many other close calls go unreported. 

So, next time you’re in Yellowstone, keep your distance from the bison. They might look docile, but they’re not your friends. And if they start to approach, move away. The last thing you want is to end up in the hospital after a bison attack.
