Imagine a robot intern that can actually do your job. Flexion Robotics, a startup founded by ex-Nvidia engineers, has developed a humanoid robot that can handle tasks like opening doors, climbing stairs, and carrying boxes. The key to their success is a clever training method that combines simulation and limited human instruction.

Flexion’s approach is revolutionary because it uses AI to teach robots individual skills, then lets a master algorithm figure out how to use them in real-world scenarios. The software digests videos of humans performing tasks, learns from simulations, and applies those skills autonomously. This means the robot can navigate an office, retrieve a parcel, and unpack it without constant human oversight.

The real game here is the AI, not the robot itself. Nikita Rudin, Flexion’s CEO, emphasizes that reinforcement learning is the secret sauce, allowing the software to master tasks through trial and error. This approach could make humanoids more versatile and efficient, potentially replacing human labor in various industries.

But success isn’t guaranteed. Flexion faces stiff competition and needs to work closely with hardware manufacturers to make their software commercially viable. Still, the potential market for robot foundation models is massive, estimated to be worth $150 billion by 2036. If Flexion can crack the code, they might just have the future of robotics on their hands.
