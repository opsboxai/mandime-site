The Scout Traveler SUV just got a whole lot cooler with the Spirit of '26 makeover. This isn’t just a fresh coat of paint; it’s a nod to the iconic Spirit of '76 package that International Harvester rolled out for the U.S. bicentennial. The original was a head-turner, with its Winter White paint, red and blue stripes, and Wedgwood Blue interior. Now, Scout has reimagined it for the Traveler, sticking to a tasteful, subtle homage that avoids the gaudy flash of many modern throwbacks.

The Spirit of '26 isn’t just about aesthetics; it’s a celebration of history and a clever blend of nostalgia and modernity. The stripes are abstracted to fit the new body lines, and the Harvester graphics replace the old ski team crest. This approach makes it feel timeless and broadens its appeal beyond just the history buffs. It’s a look that could easily fit into any garage, whether you’re a fan of vintage Americana or just someone who appreciates a well-executed design.

While Scout hasn’t announced plans to offer this package on the production Traveler, they’ve at least given us a taste with a capsule collection that includes everything from Igloo coolers to hats and tees. It’s a solid reminder that sometimes, a little bit of history can go a long way in making a modern vehicle feel a whole lot cooler.
