Major changes are looming for Microsoft’s gaming division after years of bad decisions. Let’s face it, Xbox is in a dire state, and the fallout from some disastrous moves is about to get very ugly.

It wasn’t always this way. The original Xbox in 2001 seemed like a solid contender, with Halo and Xbox Live helping to solidify its position. But things went downhill with the Xbox One in 2013, thanks to a misguided push into non-gaming features. The Xbox Series X/S generation only made things worse, with confusing marketing and a costly pivot to subscription services like Game Pass.

The company spent billions on Game Pass, hoping to hit 100 million subscribers by 2030. Instead, it plateaued at around 30 million. Meanwhile, the “This is an Xbox” campaign only added to the confusion. The Activision Blizzard deal, costing $68.7 billion, didn’t help either.

Now, with new CEO Asha Sharma at the helm, Xbox is facing a “reset” that includes layoffs, studio closures, and game cancellations. The irony is that some of the most talented and beloved studios, like Arkane and Double Fine, are being hit the hardest. These are the teams behind iconic games like Dishonored and Psychonauts, and now they’re being discarded because of poor decisions they had no part in.

The video game industry is already in a rough spot, with expensive hardware, gutted studios, and anti-consumer behavior. But Xbox’s issues feel particularly existential. Its hardware and subscription businesses are faltering, and now it’s decimating its game development teams. The layoffs are expected to start next week, and it’s not clear just how widespread they will be.

In short, Xbox is a shell of its former self, and the coming months will be a test of whether it can recover from its missteps.
