Waze is about to get a whole lot smarter, thanks to Google's AI assistant, Gemini. The driving app is rolling out a suite of new features that aim to make your commutes more personalized and less annoying.

First up, Waze is integrating Gemini to handle voice commands more naturally. Now, you can report traffic incidents or suggest map updates with conversational ease. No more fumbling with buttons or trying to remember specific commands. Just say it like you mean it. And if you're looking for a coffee shop or a gas station, you can ask Waze to find it for you in plain English.

But let's not forget the non-AI updates. Waze is adding a "less chatty" mode for voice prompts, which is a godsend for those of us who want to listen to our music or podcasts without interruptions. Plus, there's a new Motorcycle Mode that takes into account two-wheeled shortcuts and more accurate ETAs, making it a win for riders.

Lastly, Waze will start suggesting routes based on your past trips and local traffic patterns. So if you're a highway junkie, Waze will prioritize those routes for you. It's like having a personal traffic consultant in your car.

Google is clearly stepping up its game with Waze, but it's still playing catch-up to its own Google Maps, which has been getting more AI-powered features over time. Still, these updates are a big win for anyone who wants a smarter, more personalized driving experience.
