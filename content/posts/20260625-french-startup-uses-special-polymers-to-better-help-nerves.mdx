Imagine slicing an avocado and accidentally cutting your finger. Now, imagine a startup that could help heal that nerve injury with a biodegradable liquid. French firm Tissium has developed a groundbreaking polymer that could revolutionize nerve repair.

Peripheral nerve injuries are no joke. Whether it's a woodworking accident or a clumsy kitchen mishap, these injuries can leave you with a lifetime of tingling, numbness, or even excruciating pain. But Tissium's liquid solution, made from fatty acids and glycerol, could change that. The biopolymer acts like a splint, holding severed nerves in place while they heal. Once the healing process is complete, the material biodegrades, leaving your nerves intact.

In a recent trial with 12 patients, all participants regained their ability to feel temperature, pain, texture, and light touch in their fingers. That's a significant improvement over the 80 percent success rate with traditional methods. And the best part? None of the patients reported any pain or complications a year later.

Tissium's technology isn't just limited to nerve injuries. The company is also exploring its use in hernia repairs, where it can provide consistency and improve recovery outcomes. Surgeons have been able to apply the treatment 100 percent of the time, with patients showing signs of improved quality of life, lower pain levels, and a reduced recurrence rate of hernias.

The startup has already raised €60 million in funding, with plans to expand into cardiovascular reconstruction. This new technology could be a game-changer for men who are always pushing the limits, whether in the workshop or the kitchen. No more worrying about that slip of the knife, Tissium's liquid could have your nerves back to full strength in no time.
