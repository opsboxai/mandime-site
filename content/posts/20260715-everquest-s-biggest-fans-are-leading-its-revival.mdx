Travel was a massive pain point in the original EverQuest. Remember those endless treks across Norrath, where you’d spend hours just walking from one zone to another? The devs behind EverQuest Legends are bringing back that nostalgia, but with a twist. They’re adding quality-of-life tweaks to make the journey a bit smoother, while still preserving the essence of what made the original so memorable.

EverQuest Legends is set to release on July 28th, and it’s a revival that’s been in the making for over 25 years. The game is a reimagining of the original MMORPG from 1999, with a team of dedicated fans-turned-developers at the helm. Sean “Rogean” Norton, a veteran of the Project 1999 emulation community, is now a senior engineer on Legends. His experience with preserving the original game’s spirit is invaluable, ensuring that Legends feels like a true throwback.

The team is going to great lengths to stay true to the original look and feel of EverQuest. They’ve dug through old server directories and CD-ROMs to reintroduce elements like the original MIDI music, spell effects, and character models. This level of dedication to preservation is a win for game history, especially as more titles become digital-only and harder to archive.

While Legends isn’t just about nostalgia, the team is careful not to overstep. They’ve made quality-of-life tweaks, like adding the Gather Party ability to make it easier to join friends in battle, but they’ve also ensured that these changes don’t overshadow the original experience. For instance, they increased the reuse timer for Gather Party to keep foot travel and other modes of transportation meaningful.

In a world where live-service games and their developers are often gutted or shut down, EverQuest Legends stands out as a beacon of hope. It’s a testament to the power of dedicated fans and the enduring appeal of a game that defined a generation. If you’re a nostalgic gamer or just curious about the revival of a classic, Legends is worth your attention.
