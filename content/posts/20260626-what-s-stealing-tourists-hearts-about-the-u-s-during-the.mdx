thousands of tourists are pouring into the u.s. for the world cup, and they're falling hard for some of the most mundane aspects of american life. mark strassmann from CBS Evening News breaks down what's stealing their hearts.

the video opens with a shot of a bustling street in a major u.s. city, filled with tourists snapping photos and sipping on cold drinks. strassmann explains that while americans might take these everyday experiences for granted, they're a revelation for many visitors. "from the convenience of drive-thrus to the sprawling landscapes of national parks, tourists are discovering a side of america that's far removed from the sports action," strassmann says.

one of the biggest surprises for tourists is the sheer size and diversity of the country. "people are amazed by the vastness of the u.s.," strassmann notes. "from the towering skyscrapers of new york to the serene beauty of the grand canyon, there's something for everyone." the video features interviews with tourists who express their awe at the variety of landscapes and the ease of travel across the country.

another standout feature is the american food scene. "drive-thrus, diners, and food trucks are all big hits," strassmann adds. "visitors are blown away by the speed and variety of american cuisine." the video showcases a group of tourists enjoying a meal at a classic american diner, with wide-eyed expressions that speak volumes about their delight.

strassmann also highlights the friendliness of americans. "despite the stereotypes, many tourists find americans to be incredibly welcoming and helpful," he says. "from locals offering directions to impromptu conversations in public spaces, the warmth of american hospitality is a big draw."

the video concludes with a montage of tourists sharing their experiences, from hiking in the great outdoors to exploring urban centers. "for many, the world cup is just the beginning of a love affair with america," strassmann concludes. "and who knows, some might even decide to stay."

CBS Evening News put this one through the ringer, offering a fresh perspective on what makes the u.s. a unique and captivating destination for tourists.
