The 1987 300ZX landed right when Nissan figured out how to make a sports car that didn't feel like a punishment. At 8,500 bucks, this one is asking for the kind of money that used to mean something, which means whoever's selling it knows what they have.

The Z32 generation wouldn't show up until 1990, so this is the last of the Z31s, the ones that actually aged into something desirable instead of being forgotten in a garage. These cars are genuinely quick for their era, twinturbo performance that still threatens modern stuff in the right hands, and the design language aged better than almost any 80s car that wasn't Italian. The fact that this example has been "tenderly treated" matters. A 37-year-old Z that wasn't beaten on is already rare. A clean, sorted example at eight grand sits in that weird pocket where it's cheap enough to actually drive without losing sleep, but expensive enough that you probably shouldn't drive it that hard anyway.

The real question is whether the mechanicals match the cosmetics. That's where these cars either sing or drain your bank account. A Z31 with fresh belts, a solid transmission, and numbers matching everything under the hood is a ticket to something nobody else at the Cars and Coffee has. Without that foundation, you're buying a beautiful problem. The asking price suggests they think it's sorted, but that's exactly what everyone's seller thinks.
