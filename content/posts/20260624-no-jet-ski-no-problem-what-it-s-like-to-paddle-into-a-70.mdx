Tikanui Smith, a Tahitian big-wave surfer, is on a mission to paddle into the biggest wave ever surfed. Currently, the record stands at 63 feet, set by Aaron Gold in 2016. Smith is being followed by an HBO documentary crew for his attempts, and his latest attempt in Nazare, Portugal, was a heart-stopping affair.

Smith paddled into a 70-foot wave, but the moment he realized he wasn’t going to catch it, he found himself in the impact zone. The safety jetski driver came in fast, hitting his board. With mere seconds to react, Smith grabbed the sled, trying to put his 12-foot board on it. The wave broke, and he found himself in a life-threatening situation.

Smith’s journey to this point has been fraught with challenges. In 2018, he was held under by four successive waves at the World Surf League’s Big Wave Challenge in Nazare. He nearly died, but that experience solidified his belief that it was his destiny to paddle into the biggest wave. A subsequent mountain bike accident nearly paralyzed him, and he retired, dropping his sponsors and starting a tourism business in Mo’orea.

But his passion for big-wave surfing never faded. In 2024, he sought hypnotherapy to confront his fear of quitting. The therapist told him, “You’re not ready to retire. Surfing is in you. You are a phoenix, rising from the ashes.” That August, he started training to break the record.

When Smith returned to Nazare in February, he slept in a container in the harbor for three weeks, with no funds. Despite the hardships, he persevered, and Garrett McNamara, a fellow surfer, praised his dedication.

The wipeout in Nazare was brutal. Smith was held under for twenty seconds, and a second wave broke on him, causing him to feel something break on his heart side and belly. He was convinced he was going to die, but he emerged, cracked ribs and all, with a renewed sense of purpose.

Smith’s journey is a testament to his resilience and his deep connection to the ocean. He believes it’s his destiny to break the record, and he’s not backing down. After his near-death experience, he sought guidance from a tahu’a, a visionary, who told him, “You are an ocean warrior. Your animal is the whale. You’re protected by a whale.”

Smith’s story is a reminder that pushing the limits can be terrifying, but it’s what makes life worth living. He’s not afraid to die, but he’s not ready to give up on his dream. He’ll be back in Nazare in September, ready to chase the biggest wave ever.
