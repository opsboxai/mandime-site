Turner Allen, a personal stylist who's helped rebuild dozens of wardrobes, has a simple rule: do an underwear audit every 12 months. It's a straightforward way to ensure you're not walking around in a pair of boxers that's seen better days. 

Think about it: how many times have you found yourself in a situation where you wished you had fresh, clean, and well-fitting underwear? The lifespan of your skivvies depends on a few factors, like how often you wear them and how you wash them. If you rotate through a smaller number of pairs, they'll get washed more frequently and wear out faster. 

Allen suggests owning around 14 pairs to get you through a couple of weeks without laundry. That's a solid middle ground, but if you're a minimalist, you might need to wash more often. On the flip side, if you have a larger collection, you can space out your laundry days. 

To extend the life of your underwear, treat them like your favorite t-shirt: wash in cold water and hang dry. This can make a huge difference, especially for cotton blends. And if you're looking for a smooth, barely-there feel, Allen recommends brands like Classic Fella or CDLP, which use materials like modal and Lyocell to keep things comfortable and long-lasting.

The key is to stay vigilant. Keep an eye out for holes, stains, or sagging, and don't wait until it's too late. A quick annual check and a few new pairs here and there will keep your underwear drawer in top shape. Trust us, your future self will thank you.
