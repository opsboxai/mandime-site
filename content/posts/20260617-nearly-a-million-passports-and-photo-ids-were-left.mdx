Finding nearly a million unprotected passports and photo IDs online is like stumbling upon a gold mine of personal data. Typing a few letters and numbers into a web browser, Sean Hollister uncovered a massive security lapse involving identity documents of complete strangers. The breach exposed everything from German passports to Spanish IDs, with personal details and photos left wide open for anyone to see. This isn't just a privacy nightmare; it's a serious security risk that could lead to identity theft and fraud. The culprit? A cannabis club management system called Nefos, which left its database exposed without any password protection. The takeaway? Always double-check your security settings, especially when handling sensitive information. This breach shows just how vulnerable our personal data can be when proper safeguards aren't in place.
