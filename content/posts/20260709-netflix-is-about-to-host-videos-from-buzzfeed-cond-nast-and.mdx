Starting on August 3rd, Netflix is about to get a whole lot more diverse. The streaming giant is adding video content from a slew of digital media brands, including BuzzFeed, Condé Nast, and Hearst Magazines. This means you'll be able to watch everything from Architectural Digest’s “Open Door” to Vanity Fair’s “Lie Detector Test” right alongside your favorite shows.

But why should you care? Well, for one, it's a convenient way to get your fix of food, travel, fashion, and more without having to jump between apps. And with Netflix's recent struggles in retaining viewers for second seasons, this move could be a smart way to keep subscribers engaged with fresh, bite-sized content. 

Plus, it's a win for content creators too. These brands are shifting their focus from YouTube to a platform with a more captive audience. So, while you might miss some of the comments and community vibe of YouTube, you'll get a curated selection of quality content right where you already spend your streaming time.
