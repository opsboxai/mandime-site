Xbox is about to get a major shake-up, and it's not pretty. On June 10th, CEO Asha Sharma and chief content officer Matt Booty sent a memo to staff warning of an “Xbox reset.” The memo outlined some serious challenges, including a 3 percent “accountability margin” and a “hardware component crisis” that’s driving up prices. The memo also hinted at some big changes, including potential studio closures and layoffs.

The Verge and Bloomberg reported that Microsoft is planning to lay off a significant chunk of its Xbox division, with cuts expected to be announced on July 6th. The company is reportedly looking at closing at least five studios, including the one behind Marvel’s Blade. This isn’t just about cutting costs; it’s about rethinking the entire Xbox strategy.

Under Sharma’s leadership, Xbox has already made some major changes. Prices for Xbox consoles are going up, with the Series S hitting $499.99 and the Series X hitting $749.99. Game Pass prices have been lowered, but new Call of Duty games are no longer included. The company has also rebranded to XBOX and made some titles exclusive to the console.

But the real shake-up is in the works. IO Interactive, the studio behind Hitman, is laying off staff after a partnership on its next big project, Project Fantasy, fell through. Meanwhile, Ninja Theory, the studio behind Hellblade, is closing down, and other studios like Compulsion Games and Double Fine are in talks to spin off.

This “reset” isn’t just about cutting costs; it’s about rethinking Xbox’s entire approach. The company is facing a perfect storm of challenges, from rising component prices to an overextended studio system. The moves being made now are about survival, but they’re also about positioning Xbox for the future. 

If you’re a gamer, this means a lot of uncertainty. But it also means a chance for Xbox to come back stronger. The changes are tough, but they’re necessary. Stay tuned for more updates as this story unfolds.
