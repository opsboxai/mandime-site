Demis Hassabis, the Google DeepMind CEO and cofounder, thinks the world needs a global AI watchdog, and he wants it to be led by the US. In a recent blog post, Hassabis argues that this watchdog should have the power to hit the brakes on AI models if they become too dangerous. Given the US's economic and technical standing, Hassabis believes it's the best place to set these global standards.

The idea of a global AI watchdog isn't just a pipe dream; Hassabis has been quietly building support for months, briefing the Trump administration, other AI labs, and European officials. He's hoping to have this organization up and running before the end of the year. The urgency stems from the rapid advancement of AI systems, with Hassabis suggesting that artificial general intelligence (AGI) is just a few years away. When we look back on this time, he argues, we'll realize we were standing in the foothills of the singularity, a new age for humanity.

But why should you care? Well, AI isn't just about fancy algorithms and robots; it's about the future of our economy, our security, and our very way of life. Hassabis and other industry leaders are pushing for a coherent framework to govern AI, and with good reason. Without it, we risk unleashing technologies that could have catastrophic consequences. The stakes are high, and the time to act is now.
