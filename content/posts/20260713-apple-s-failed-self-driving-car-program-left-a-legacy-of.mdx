Apple's self-driving car program might have been a dud, but it left a powerful legacy: the Neural Engine. This isn't just some fancy tech buzzword; it's the heart of Apple's on-device AI processing. The Neural Engine first showed up in the iPhone X with the A11 Bionic chip, initially powering features like FaceID and Animoji. But it wasn't just about flashy gimmicks; it was about laying down a solid foundation for AI processing right on the device.

Fast-forward to today, and Apple is doubling down on its AI hardware. The company is skipping the usual Pro, Max, and Ultra versions of its upcoming M6 chip and instead focusing on the M7 Ultra, set to drop in 2027. This beast of a chip is expected to support up to 1.5TB of RAM, making it a serious contender for server-grade applications. The Neural Engine upgrades in the M7 Ultra are a big deal, not just for Apple's hardware but also for its privacy stance. By processing more data locally, Apple can claim to be more secure and private, since less data needs to be sent to the cloud.

So, while Apple's self-driving car dreams may have stalled, the tech it developed is now powering some of the most advanced AI chips on the market. It's a reminder that sometimes, the most significant innovations come from projects that don't quite make it to the finish line.
