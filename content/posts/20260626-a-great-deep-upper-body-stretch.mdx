Chris Bumstead, aka "The Mountain," knows a thing or two about building a solid upper body. In this video, he breaks down a deep upper body stretch that's perfect for anyone looking to loosen up those tight muscles. The stretch targets your shoulders, chest, and upper back, hitting all the spots that tend to get stiff after a hard workout.

The move starts with you standing tall, arms at your sides. You then bring one arm across your chest, using your other hand to gently pull it closer, feeling the stretch in your shoulder and upper back. Bumstead emphasizes the importance of breathing deeply and holding the stretch for at least 30 seconds on each side. 

What's great about this stretch is its simplicity and effectiveness. It's not just about feeling good; it can help improve your range of motion and prevent injuries. Bumstead's no-nonsense approach makes it easy to follow along, and his background in powerlifting adds credibility to the technique.

If you're someone who spends a lot of time at the gym or in front of a computer, this stretch can be a game changer. It's a quick and effective way to unwind and feel better, all while looking like you know what you're doing. Give it a try and see how much better you feel.
