If you're looking to build chiseled shoulders, you need to hit all three heads of the deltoids: anterior, medial, and posterior. Dean Stattmann breaks down the essentials to get you there.

First up, the anterior deltoid, which is the front part of your shoulder muscle. The overhead press is a solid choice for this area, offering functional carryover and strength. If you're dealing with elbow pain, opt for the dumbbell front raise instead. This exercise allows you to use lighter weights, making it easier on your joints while still targeting the front delts effectively.

Moving on to the medial deltoid, or the middle part of your shoulders. The lateral raise is a classic move, but using dumbbells can often lead to uneven tension. Instead, try the cable lateral raise. This exercise keeps consistent tension on the muscle throughout the full range of motion, ensuring you get the most out of each rep. If grip strength is an issue, consider using an ankle strap to focus solely on the mind-muscle connection.

For the posterior deltoid, or the rear part of your shoulders, the reverse fly is your go-to move. This exercise isolates the posterior deltoids, which are often neglected in standard shoulder workouts. You can perform this with a cable station or dumbbells, but a machine with bent arms and a pad for driving your elbows back is ideal. This setup ensures your hands aren’t the limiting factor, allowing you to focus on the muscle itself.

Lastly, don’t forget about your rotator cuff. While it may not be Instagram-worthy, maintaining this stabilizing muscle is crucial for shoulder longevity and injury prevention. The face pull is a great rotational exercise that adds 3D motion to your routine. Ensure you use a long cable attachment or two separate handles to achieve full external rotation.

By incorporating these exercises into your routine, you’ll hit all the necessary areas of your shoulders, ensuring balanced and chiseled development.
