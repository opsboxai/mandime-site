Releasing sterilized flies might sound like a bizarre solution, but it's the US's plan to combat the flesh-eating screwworm. Emily Mullin breaks down how this method works: by overwhelming the local population with sterilized males, the screwworm population crashes. The idea is simple, but the execution isn't. The US currently lacks the capacity to produce enough sterilized flies to effectively manage outbreaks. This plan shows just how creative and sometimes unexpected the solutions to pest control can be.
