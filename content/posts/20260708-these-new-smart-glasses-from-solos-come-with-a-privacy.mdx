Solos just dropped a new pair of smart glasses with a twist: a privacy shield for the cameras. The AirGo V2, priced at $299, comes with a camera but also offers a Privacy Kit that lets you clip on a cover to block the camera. This move is a direct response to the growing concern about privacy in the age of smart glasses.

The idea of a privacy shield sounds great in theory, but it's a bit of a double-edged sword. On one hand, it gives you the option to disable the camera when you need to, which is a big deal for those worried about being recorded without consent. On the other hand, it's an extra step that might deter people from actually using it. Who wants to fumble with a clip-on shield every time they enter a no-camera zone?

Solos isn't the only player in this game. Meta has been the big dog in the smart glasses market, but other tech giants like Google and Samsung are getting in on the action. Even Apple is reportedly working on its own pair of smart glasses. The market is heating up, and privacy is becoming a key selling point.

If you're in the market for smart glasses, Solos' approach might be worth considering. But remember, it's not just about the tech, it's about how you use it. Keep that privacy shield handy, and you might just find a balance between convenience and security.
