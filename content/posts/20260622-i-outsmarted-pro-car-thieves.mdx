Mark Rober, the guy who used to work on NASA projects, outsmarted a group of professional car thieves with a DIY security system. In his latest video, he details how he set up a series of traps and sensors to catch the thieves in the act. 

Rober installed a fake car with a camera system and a network of pressure sensors under the tires. When the thieves tried to jack the car, they triggered the sensors, and Rober and his team were alerted. The video shows the thieves' confusion and frustration as they encounter one obstacle after another, from fake license plates to a car that won't start.

What's most impressive is Rober's commitment to public safety. Instead of just catching the thieves, he worked with local law enforcement to ensure they were apprehended. The video also includes tips on how to protect your own vehicle, like using a steering wheel lock and parking in well-lit areas.

This isn't just a fun prank; it's a reminder that with a bit of ingenuity and tech know-how, you can significantly boost your car's security. And if you ever find yourself dealing with car thieves, maybe you can take a page from Rober's book and turn the tables on them.
