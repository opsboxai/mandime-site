Opinel just dropped a trio of high-performance knives designed for specific outdoor environments, and they’re a far cry from the classic wood-handled models you might be used to. The new Outdoor Collection includes knives tailored for mountain, trail, and marine tasks, each with specialized features that make them stand out.

The Summit model, developed with elite instructors from Chamonix’s National School of Skiing and Mountaineering (ENSA), is a minimalist tool designed for vertical rock, ice, and backcountry snow. Its aggressively serrated blade and protruding tip for one-handed opening make it a standout for alpine adventures. This knife is a departure from the traditional Opinel, with a focus on functionality and durability in harsh conditions.

The Horizon, while still reminiscent of the classic Opinel design, features a reinforced polyamide handle that can withstand moisture, impacts, and extreme temperatures from -40°C to +80°C. The vibrant orange color ensures it won’t get lost in your backpack, and the built-in whistle adds an extra layer of safety for emergency situations.

The Marine model is built for life on deck, with robust stainless steel that resists corrosion from salt spray. Its aggressive partial serration allows for quick rope cuts, and the smooth belly offers versatility. But the real standout feature is the built-in marlinspike, which can wedge open stubborn knots, making it an invaluable tool for anyone working with lines.

These knives aren’t just about aesthetics; they’re about performance and practicality. Whether you’re scaling a mountain, hiking a trail, or navigating the deck of a boat, Opinel’s new Outdoor Collection has you covered. The advanced polymers and specialized coatings ensure these knives can handle whatever the elements throw at them, making them a must-have for any serious outdoor enthusiast.
