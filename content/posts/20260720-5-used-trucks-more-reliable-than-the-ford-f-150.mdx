The Ford F-150 sells more trucks than anything else in America, which tells you something about marketing and brand loyalty but almost nothing about whether you should actually buy one used. Steven Mike Voser dug into this at Jalopnik and found five trucks that consistently outlast the F-150 in real-world reliability, and the gap is wider than you'd think.

The thing is, sales numbers create a weird gravity well. Everyone buys F-150s, so everyone assumes they're bulletproof. But ownership forums and repair data tell a different story. Certain used trucks—especially from brands that don't have the same household name recognition—rack up way fewer major issues per mile, cost less to fix when something does break, and hold up through 200k miles with less drama. That matters if you're actually planning to drive this thing into the ground instead of trading it in every five years.

The article breaks down specific models, years, and what makes them stand out against Ford's juggernaut. If you're in the market for a used truck, this is the kind of contrarian data that saves you from a $40k mistake and puts you in something that actually runs.
