If you're prepping for the Mad Max apocalypse, you're probably thinking about how to outrun the chaos. But let's get real: the first thing to go is likely to be the fuel. Gasoline has a shelf life, and in a post-apocalyptic world, you can't just swing by the pump. So, what's your plan?

Plenty of you suggested electric vehicles with solar power. It's a smart move, solar panels can last for decades, and with the right setup, you can keep your EV running without worrying about fuel shortages. A solar-powered EV might not be as flashy as a souped-up V8, but it's practical and sustainable. 

Others opted for diesel vehicles, like the VW TDI or a vintage Unimog, which can run on a variety of fuels, including heating oil. This flexibility could be a lifesaver in a world where fuel is scarce. And let's not forget the old-school approach: a mountain bike with the right setup can get you far, with no batteries or fuel to worry about.

But here's the kicker: the lone wolf approach is a myth. In a Mad Max world, you're not going to survive alone. Communities are key. Whether it's a group of survivors with a makeshift refinery or a band of travelers with a shared goal, you need people on your side. So, while you're picking out your apocalypse ride, remember: it's not just about the vehicle; it's about who's riding with you.
