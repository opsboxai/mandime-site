McLaren just pulled a major nostalgia trip by reviving a design that Bruce McLaren himself sketched out decades ago. The M6GT, set to debut at the Goodwood Festival of Speed, isn't just a throwback; it's a meticulous recreation that took over 3,000 hours to build. The MSO team dug through McLaren's archives, finding original molds and blueprints to create a car that's as period-correct as it gets.

The M6GT is a one-off tribute to McLaren's founder, and it's a damn impressive piece of work. The body was built using those original molds, and the engine and gearbox were recreated to match the specs from the '60s. The chassis comes from a period-built McLaren M6A Can Am race car, and the body was constructed with materials and techniques true to the era. Even the closed-dome aluminum rivets were installed by a specialist from the aerospace industry, ensuring every detail is spot-on.

The car's design is pure '60s race car for the road, with a low profile, mid-engine layout, and big swooping front fenders. The headlights and taillights are so simple they look like afterthoughts, and the nose is so low it could scoop bugs off the pavement. The interior is equally vintage, with green leather, old-school Jaeger gauges, and a walnut shift knob that looks like it belongs in a classic British sports car.

But the real kicker is the unique McLaren badge with the vintage font and the adorable kiwi bird, paying homage to Bruce McLaren's New Zealand roots. This car isn't just a nod to the past; it's a celebration of McLaren's heritage, and it's going to make a huge splash at Goodwood.
