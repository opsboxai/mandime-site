Bryan Johnson, the Silicon Valley billionaire known for his quest to live forever, recently announced he has an incurable autoimmune disease. The internet's reaction? A mix of schadenfreude and finger-pointing, with wellness influencers quick to blame his extreme biohacking practices.

But let's pause for a moment. Johnson's journey, while extreme, isn't entirely unique. Many of us have found ourselves in a similar situation, albeit on a smaller scale. You start with a basic fitness tracker, noticing something off with your health, and before you know it, you're down a rabbit hole of supplements, blood tests, and endless data analysis. Johnson's story is a stark reminder of how far we'll go to understand and optimize our bodies, especially when conventional medicine fails to provide clear answers.

The irony is that in our quest for perfect health, we often end up creating more stress and confusion. Johnson's diagnosis, despite his millions and team of doctors, took years to uncover. For many of us, it's a similar struggle, with wearables and health apps offering more questions than answers. The real takeaway here isn't to mock Johnson's methods but to recognize the complexity of our bodies and the limits of our current health tech. So, the next time you see someone sharing their latest health hack, remember: it's a journey, not a destination.
