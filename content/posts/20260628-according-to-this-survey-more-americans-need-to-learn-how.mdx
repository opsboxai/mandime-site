Only 3% of Americans are both confident and correct about how to escape a sinking car. That's a terrifying stat, considering that vehicular drownings account for 1% of all traffic fatalities in the US. The Cooperative Election Study (CCES) surveyed 1,000 adults and found that most people are clueless about the right method of escape. 

The survey revealed that less than half of those surveyed knew the correct method: escaping through a rear side window. And shockingly, almost two-thirds of respondents thought they had more time to figure out the problem than they actually do. In reality, you usually have just one minute to act before the car is fully submerged. 

The SWOC method, recommended by Kids and Car Safety, is your best bet. SWOC stands for Seat belts off, Window open, Out immediately, Children first. The rear side window is crucial because cars typically sink nose-first, making it harder to open the front doors. Once you've unbuckled and opened the rear window, get everyone out as quickly as possible, prioritizing children. 

The Mythbusters episode where Adam and Jamie tested this method in a pool underscores just how difficult it is to open a car door underwater. They found that automatic windows can still function for a while after submersion, making the SWOC method reliable even in newer cars. 

So, the next time you're driving near water, remember SWOC. It could save your life.
