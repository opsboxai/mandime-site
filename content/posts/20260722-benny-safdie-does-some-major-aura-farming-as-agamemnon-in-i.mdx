Benny Safdie got cast as Agamemnon in Christopher Nolan's The Odyssey, and he went full method on the Trojan War general. The guy who made Uncut Gems and Good Time isn't exactly known for playing kings, but he leaned hard into the aggressive physicality the role demanded. He's talking about how he embraced that side of the character, the kind of raw, commanding presence you need when you're supposed to be leading men into battle for a decade straight.

What's interesting is how Safdie approaches a role like this versus his directorial work. When he's behind the camera, he's orchestrating chaos and tension through performance and editing. Playing Agamemnon means being the source of that tension instead. The character's got to carry weight, authority, and the kind of barely contained violence that comes from a man who's spent ten years at war and isn't quite finished yet. Safdie's never been a conventional leading man in anything he's done, so casting him as a classical king is genuinely unexpected. That's probably exactly why Nolan picked him.
