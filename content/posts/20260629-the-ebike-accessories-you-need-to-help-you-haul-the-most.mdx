An unadorned ebike is a blank canvas, and Michael Venutolo-Mantovani shows us how to turn it into a cargo-hauling beast. When you’re looking to maximize your ebike’s capabilities, the key is in the accessories. Take the Radwagon 4, for example. It started as a family-friendly ebike with a front basket, a rear pad seat, and a Thule Yepp 2 Maxi seat for the kids. But as your family grows, so do your needs. 

Now, imagine transforming that same ebike into a Grocery Getter. The secret lies in the panniers. Aventon’s Abound Pannier Set, for instance, can hold nearly 90 pounds of cargo, with 44 pounds per pannier. That’s enough to fit a week’s worth of groceries for a family of four, evenly distributed between the two sides. And if you’re in a rainy region, waterproof cargo bags can protect your haul from the elements.

But it’s not just about the cargo. Safety and comfort are paramount, especially when you’re transporting kids. A steel cage like Rad’s Caboose can secure the seats, and runner boards ensure little legs aren’t dangling. As your kids grow, you can swap out the seats for additional cargo bags, giving you even more storage options. 

Whether you’re building a Family Wagon or a Grocery Getter, the right accessories can turn your ebike into a versatile, reliable mode of transportation. So, get ready to haul the most stuff, and do it in style.
