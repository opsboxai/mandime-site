Imagine a reality show where anything goes, and the line between entertainment and mayhem blurs. Fishtank is that unhinged future of reality TV, and WIRED dove headfirst into the chaos. 

Set in a sealed environment, Fishtank pushes the boundaries of interactive, violent, and gross content. Think Big Brother but with no rules, no limits, and no holds barred. The participants are free to do whatever they want, and viewers get to watch every twisted moment unfold.

WIRED's on-location coverage offers a glimpse into this bizarre world, where reality TV meets survivalist tactics and extreme behavior. The show's creators have crafted a space where the participants are left to their own devices, and the results are as shocking as they are captivating.

For fans of edgy content and those who can stomach the raw, unfiltered nature of Fishtank, this is a must-watch. But be warned: it's not for the faint of heart. The show's unapologetic approach to pushing boundaries makes it a cult hit, and WIRED's coverage captures the essence of its unorthodox appeal.
