In a World Cup penalty shootout, the team that kicks first is often seen as having a psychological edge. But is it really that simple? According to a recent study published in Football Studies, the advantage might not be about who kicks first, but rather about the type of pressure each team faces.

For years, soccer players and coaches have believed that the team that takes the first penalty kick has a significant advantage. A study from 2010 reported that teams that started the shootout won nearly 60 percent of the time. However, as more data and research piled up, that advantage started to fade. The most comprehensive analysis to date, based on nearly 7,000 shootouts and 74,000 shots, found no significant difference in winning rates between the first and second teams.

The new study suggests that the key lies in understanding the different types of pressure. Penalty kicks where a miss means immediate elimination are much more stressful than those where a goal secures victory. The study found that when a miss means immediate elimination, the success rate drops to 60.4 percent, compared to 89.1 percent for those securing victory. This means that the team taking the second kick faces more high-pressure situations, skewing the results.

So, the next time you're watching a penalty shootout, remember that it's not just about who kicks first. The real advantage might lie in how well a team handles those critical moments of pressure. And if you're a coach, it might be worth saving your best players for those high-stakes kicks.
