Soccer players bending shots in midair isn't just magic—it's physics. Rhett Allain breaks down the science behind those iconic goals. The key is the spin on the ball, creating something called the Magnus effect. When a ball spins, it drags air around it, causing a pressure difference that curves its path. So next time you see a player nail a perfect curl, remember it's not just skill—it's a lesson in aerodynamics.
