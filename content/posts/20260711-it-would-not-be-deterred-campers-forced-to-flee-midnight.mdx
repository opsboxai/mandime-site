A black bear tore through a Wyoming campsite in the middle of the night, despite campers taking every precaution to avoid an attack. Shonna Dehl and Maggie Basset were camping in Bighorn National Forest when the bear invaded their tents, ignoring their attempts to scare it away with barking dogs and gunshots. The bear's aggressive behavior is so concerning that experts like Chris Servheen, a former national grizzly bear recovery coordinator, are calling for the bear to be euthanized.

This incident highlights just how unpredictable and dangerous encounters with bears can be, even when you follow all the rules. Dehl and Basset had no food or scented items in their tents, yet the bear still attacked. The bear's behavior suggests it has become too comfortable around humans, making it a potential danger to others in the future. Servheen emphasizes that bears can learn to ignore deterrents like dogs if they've had positive experiences in the past, which is exactly what happened here.

The takeaway is clear: bear safety is a collective responsibility. Even if you do everything right, someone else's carelessness can still put you in harm's way. The best defense is to stay vigilant, carry bear spray, and know how to use it. In this case, the bear's aggression happened too quickly for Dehl and Basset to use their bear spray effectively. But in future encounters, bear spray could be the difference between life and death.
