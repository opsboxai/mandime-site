Ford just recalled 740,000 trucks and SUVs, and here's the kicker: these vehicles could roll away even when parked. That's right, the parking pawl, which is supposed to keep your ride stationary, might not be doing its job. The affected models include the 2018-2021 Ford Explorer, Expedition, and F-150, as well as the Lincoln Aviator and Navigator. 

The issue stems from a transmission fault that can damage the parking pawl, making it ineffective. Until Ford updates the Powertrain Control Modules and inspects the park system, you're better off engaging the parking brake every single time. It's a simple fix, but it's a reminder of how crucial these small components are to your safety. 

This recall is a wake-up call for all of us to double-check our vehicle's systems, especially if you're driving one of these models. It's a hassle, but it's better to be safe than sorry. Ford is handling the repairs, but in the meantime, don't take any chances. Keep that parking brake engaged, and stay vigilant.
