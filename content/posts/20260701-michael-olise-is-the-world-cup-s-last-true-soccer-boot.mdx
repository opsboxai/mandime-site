Michael Olise is the World Cup’s last true soccer-boot romantic. While everyone else is lacing up in whatever their sponsor demands, Olise is out there hunting down discontinued grails like a vintage sneaker fiend. 

Take the Nike Hypervenom Phantom III, for instance. First released in 2017 and long since discontinued, Olise still can’t get enough of these boots. He’s been known to wear up to 15 different pairs in training, and his love for the Hypervenom is unmatched. It’s like he’s a guy who can’t stop scouring eBay for that one jacket he missed out on in three different colors.

The kicker? Olise doesn’t have a shoe deal. He’s not getting a penny from Nike or any other brand. He’s just wearing what he likes, and that’s a rarity in professional sports. Most athletes are locked into contracts that dictate every piece of gear they wear. But Olise? He’s doing it his way, treating his boots like a favorite pair of sneakers, not just equipment.

In a sport where individuality is often confined to a tunnel fit, Olise stands out. He’s not just wearing boots; he’s making a statement. Whether it’s a vintage Hypervenom or an Adidas F50 Tunit from 2006, every choice feels entirely his own. That’s the kind of authenticity that’s hard to come by these days.
