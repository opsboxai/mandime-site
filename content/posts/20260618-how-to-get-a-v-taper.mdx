If you're looking to build a V-taper, you need to focus on widening your back and shoulders while keeping your waist tight. According to expert strength trainer Ian Burke, the key exercises include pull-ups, rows, and shoulder presses. Pull-ups are a no-brainer for back width, hitting your lats hard. Rows, both barbell and dumbbell, help fill out your upper back. And don’t forget shoulder presses to add some serious width to your upper body. Combine these with a solid diet and you’ll be well on your way to that iconic V-shape.
