Electric trucks were supposed to be the future, but the first wave of big, expensive electric trucks from Ford, GM, and Ram have flopped. The F-150 Lightning was a solid effort, but Ford eventually bailed on it. GM's mega-trucks with huge batteries and eye-popping range figures didn't hit the mark, with sales falling far short of expectations. Ram never even launched its electric truck before killing it off. 

But the story isn’t over. The next generation of electric trucks is taking shape, and they’re vastly different from their predecessors. These new models are smaller, less powerful, and much more affordable. The idea is to make electric trucks accessible to the average buyer, not just the early adopters and tech enthusiasts. 

Slate, Tello, and Ford are betting that smaller, less expensive electric trucks will succeed where the first-gen heavyweights didn’t. The market is shifting, and automakers are scrambling to get it right. The future of electric trucks is still uncertain, but one thing is clear: the industry is learning from its mistakes and adapting. Whether these new, more modest electric trucks will finally hit the sweet spot remains to be seen, but it's a move in the right direction. 

Joel Feder and his team dive deep into this evolving landscape on The Drivecast, breaking down the history, the current state of the market, and what's coming next. If you're curious about where electric trucks are headed, it's worth a listen.
