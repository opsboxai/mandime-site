Imagine you’re stuck in the middle of nowhere, tire flat, and the sun beating down. Who do you call for help? According to a recent survey by Autotrader, the answer is pretty clear: not Gen Z. In fact, globally, just 2% of drivers trust Gen Z to change a tire, and in some countries, that number drops to zero. 

It’s a harsh reality, but it’s not entirely fair. The survey also found that between 30 and 45% of Gen Z drivers feel confident in their ability to perform basic repairs, and many have successfully tackled these tasks. So, why the lack of trust? It likely comes down to a generational shift in how knowledge is acquired. While older generations learned through hands-on experience, Gen Z relies heavily on social media and AI for guidance.

But here’s the kicker: even Gen Z drivers who feel confident in their skills still lean on older generations when it comes to real-world know-how. This trust gap isn’t just about competence; it’s about the credibility that comes with years of experience. Gen X, for instance, has earned its reputation over decades, and it’s not going away anytime soon.

The survey also revealed some interesting cultural differences. In the US, 10% of drivers would attempt to fix a breakdown on their own without calling anyone, making them the most self-reliant. But in Canada, drivers are less likely to reach out to friends, family, or even breakdown services.

So, the next time you’re stranded, you might want to hope for a Gen X or Millennial to come to the rescue. And if you’re part of Gen Z, don’t sweat it, just keep learning and proving your worth.
