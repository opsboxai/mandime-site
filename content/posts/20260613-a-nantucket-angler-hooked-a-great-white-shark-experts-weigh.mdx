Elliot Sudal hooked a juvenile great white shark while fishing off the coast of Nantucket. Instead of reeling it in for a trophy, Sudal did something pretty remarkable: he freed the shark and let it swim away. The video of this encounter has sparked a lot of discussion among experts about the right way to handle such a rare catch. 

Sudal quickly wrestled the shark to the shallows, removed the hook, and dragged it back out to sea. His decision to release the shark has been praised by many, who see it as a sign of respect for marine life. 

While catching a great white shark is a rare feat, the decision to let it go is even rarer. This story shows that sometimes, the best catch is the one you let swim away.
