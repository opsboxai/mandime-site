If you're in the market for a used Mercedes that's both cool and practical, you're in for a wild ride. The Mercedes-Benz W124 500E, for instance, is a V8 super sedan that's a joint effort between Mercedes and Porsche. This beast isn't exactly falling off trees, with only 10,479 units built. That scarcity makes finding a clean one a challenge, but it's worth it for the blend of luxury and performance. The wiring can be a headache, and rust is a constant concern, but if you can find one in good condition, you'll own a piece of automotive history.

Then there's the CLK63 AMG Black Series, a car that screams "I'm not messing around." With only 700 produced globally, these are rare beasts. The M156 V8 is the heart of the car, but it's also the part that can cause you some serious headaches. Head bolts and camshafts are notorious for failing, so you'll want to have a mechanic who knows their stuff give it a thorough check. If you can find one that's been well-maintained, you'll have a car that's as intimidating as it is fast.

The E55 AMG wagon is another hidden gem. Only 126 were brought to the U.S., making it a true rarity. The M113K engine is a beast, but you'll need to be vigilant about the rest of the W211 chassis. SBC brakes, Airmatic suspension, and aging electronics are all potential trouble spots. A good one is a dream, but a cheap one is likely a trap.

Finally, the S212 E63 AMG wagon is a modern take on the same idea. With rear-biased all-wheel drive and a twin-turbo V8, it's a family car that's anything but boring. Even though more were sold compared to the E55, with just 783 units in the U.S., you're still looking at a rare find. The challenge is in finding one that's been treated right, but if you do, you'll have a car that's as practical as it is powerful.

In the end, buying a used Mercedes is a gamble, but one that can pay off big if you hit the right combination of rarity, condition, and maintenance. These cars aren't just vehicles; they're experiences. So, if you're ready to take the plunge, do your homework, and you might just end up with a legend on wheels.
