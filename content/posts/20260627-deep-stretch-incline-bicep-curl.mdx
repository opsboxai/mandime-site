Chris Bumstead, aka "Bumstead," shows off a bicep curl variation that'll make you rethink your workout routine. The deep stretch incline bicep curl isn't just about lifting weights; it's about maximizing the stretch and tension in your biceps. Bumstead starts with a bench set at a 45-degree angle, emphasizing the importance of form and the deep stretch at the bottom of the movement. 

The key here is to feel the stretch in your biceps as you lower the weights, which helps recruit more muscle fibers and can lead to better growth. Bumstead demonstrates the move with a pair of dumbbells, but you can use any weights that challenge you without compromising form. The incline angle is crucial; it shifts the focus to the biceps and helps you avoid using momentum to lift the weights.

Bumstead's video is a must-watch for anyone looking to add some variety to their arm workouts. The deep stretch incline bicep curl isn't just about lifting heavier; it's about feeling the burn and ensuring you're hitting your muscles in new ways. If you've been stuck in a rut with your bicep curls, this variation might be just what you need to break through a plateau.
