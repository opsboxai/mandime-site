Three people have had cancer-detecting implants placed in their brains. Coherence Neuro is testing a brain-computer interface that could use electrical stimulation to prevent tumor growth. This isn't just some sci-fi dream; it's happening now. The tech could revolutionize how we approach cancer treatment, shifting from reactive to proactive. Imagine having a device that can detect and potentially stop tumors before they become life-threatening. The implications are huge, and while it's early days, this could be a game-changer in the fight against cancer.
