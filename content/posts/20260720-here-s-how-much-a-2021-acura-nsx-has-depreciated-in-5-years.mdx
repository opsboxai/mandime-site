A 2021 Acura NSX that cost $156,000 new is now worth roughly $95,000 to $105,000 depending on condition and mileage. That's a hit of about 35 to 40 percent over five years, which sounds brutal until you remember what you're actually looking at. This isn't a sports car that lost value because it aged poorly or became uncool. It's a hybrid supercar with 573 horsepower, all-wheel drive, and—this is the part that matters—only 3,362 were ever made worldwide during its entire production run. The newer models from 2022 onward command similar losses because the market for a six-figure Japanese hybrid supercar is, by definition, extremely small.

The depreciation curve tells you something real about exclusivity and purpose. If you bought a 2021 NSX when they were still available, you were never buying it as a financial move. The car was always about driving something rare that most people would never see on the road. Compared to traditional supercars that hold value better, the NSX takes a bigger hit because there's less demand—it's not a Ferrari or Lamborghini with instant resale mystique, and it's not affordable enough to be a used sports car bargain. But losing 35 to 40 percent over five years on a car you actually drove is different from watching a depreciating asset sit in a garage. The owners who kept them seem to have no regrets about the experience.
