The Trump administration lifted export controls on Anthropic’s Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models, but with a twist: Anthropic had to agree to a new security measure to prevent users from accessing certain restricted capabilities. This safeguard means that any attempts to unlock those capabilities will be blocked, and the query will be processed by the less-advanced Opus 4.8 AI model instead.

This move comes after a paper by Amazon revealed a loophole that allowed users to bypass existing restrictions on Fable 5 by asking it to fix code rather than identify security issues. The administration took notice, leading to a showdown with Anthropic and the imposition of export controls. Now, with the new guardrail in place, the Commerce Department has deemed the safeguards robust enough to allow the models to be released again.

However, the saga isn’t entirely over. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has told advisers that there’s no clear path to lift the supply chain risk designation on Anthropic, which was issued in February. So while Anthropic has managed to get back into the good graces of the Commerce Department, they still have hurdles to clear with other parts of the government. It’s a reminder that in the world of AI and government oversight, nothing is ever truly settled.
