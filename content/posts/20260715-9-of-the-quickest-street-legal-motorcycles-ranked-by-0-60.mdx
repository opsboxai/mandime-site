To be both street legal and have a 0-60 time south of 3.0 seconds is the stuff of legend. Yet that's the territory each of these motorcycles treads. Let's dive into the quickest street-legal factory-built bikes, ranked by 0-60 acceleration times.

First up, the Ducati Diavel V4 RS, with a claimed 2.4-second sprint to 60 mph. While this time is unofficial, it's hard to argue with the raw power and speed of a Ducati. MotoGP champion Marc Márquez and Ducati's chief development rider, Alessandro Valia, have both clocked impressive times on this beast. But it's not just about raw speed; these bikes are about the thrill of acceleration and the confidence to handle it.

The Yamaha YZF R1, with a 2.6-second 0-60 time, is a classic example of Japanese engineering. In 2015, Cycle World recorded this time, and the bike's 998cc inline-4 engine cranks out 167.4 horsepower and 77 pound-feet of torque. The R1's performance is consistent across generations, though newer models have seen slight drops in acceleration times.

The KTM 1290 Super Duke R and BMW S1000R also hit the 2.6-second mark, showcasing the balance between power and handling. The KTM, with its 151.6 horsepower and 93.3 lb-ft of torque, is a force to be reckoned with, while the BMW offers a more balanced ride with similar performance.

The Honda CBR1000RR, another Japanese icon, also clocks in at 2.6 seconds. In a 2012 comparison, it outperformed its rivals with a 9.82-second quarter-mile run. Despite having less power than some of its competitors, the Honda's balance and handling make it a standout choice.

Lastly, the Kawasaki H2 and ZX-14R both hit the 2.6-second mark, with the H2R needing just 2.9 seconds. The H2's 998cc engine and the ZX-14R's 1,441cc engine both deliver impressive performance, making them top contenders in the world of quick street-legal bikes.

These bikes aren't just about speed; they're about the thrill of acceleration and the confidence to handle it. Whether you're a seasoned rider or just starting out, these machines offer a glimpse into the world of high-performance motorcycles.
