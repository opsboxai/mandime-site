The Fi Ultra is the first pet tracker to use SpaceX's Starlink satellites, ensuring you can track your dog anywhere in the US, even in cellular dead zones. But it's not just a tech gimmick, it's a serious tool for adventure dogs and their owners.

Built to fit any size dog, the Fi Ultra combines GPS, LTE, and now Starlink-based T-Satellite connectivity. This means if you're hiking in the middle of nowhere and your dog goes rogue, you can still track them with relative ease. During testing, the tracker connected to Starlink satellites and updated the dog's location every 2 to 3 minutes, which is about the same as a weak LTE signal. While it's not real-time, it's better than nothing when you're in the middle of nowhere.

The downside? Battery life. The Fi Ultra barely lasts two days on a charge, and it needs to be charged daily if you're going on long walks. This makes it more of an occasional-use device rather than an everyday tracker. But if you're regularly venturing into remote areas, it's worth the investment. The peace of mind it offers is invaluable, especially if you've ever had to search for a lost dog in the wilderness.

For those who live in rural areas or frequently hike in remote locations, the Fi Ultra is a solid piece of tech. Just be prepared to charge it more often than you might expect.
