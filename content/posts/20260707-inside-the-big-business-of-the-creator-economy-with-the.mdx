Inside the big business of the creator economy, with the agents making it happen.

Ali Berman and Raina Penchansky, the co-heads of UTA’s Creators division, are navigating a world where influencers and content creators are building multimillion-dollar businesses. These aren’t just social media stars; they’re running companies with diverse revenue streams, from product launches to brand deals. 

UTA, one of the biggest talent agencies in the game, has been at the forefront of this shift. Berman and Penchansky have been instrumental in shaping the digital talent division, which started in a blog-centric era and has evolved into a powerhouse representing stars like Charli D’Amelio and Markiplier. The key to their success lies in identifying up-and-coming talent and helping them build sustainable businesses. 

What’s fascinating is how these creators are moving beyond just making media. They’re launching their own products, a transition that isn’t easy for everyone. Berman and Penchansky provide the guidance and resources to make that leap, ensuring their clients aren’t just social media sensations but also savvy entrepreneurs. 

The landscape is constantly shifting, with AI and platform changes adding layers of complexity. Yet, Berman and Penchansky remain refreshingly chill about it all. They’re not just adapting; they’re thriving in a world where creators are the future of advertising. 

This interview, recorded at the Cannes Lions advertising festival, offers a deep dive into how UTA is redefining the creator economy. It’s a must-listen for anyone curious about the business side of content creation.
