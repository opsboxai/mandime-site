OpenAI is considering giving the US government a 5 percent ownership stake, a move that could be worth around $42.6 billion based on the company’s current valuation. CEO Sam Altman floated this idea to the Trump administration as a way to ease tensions and head off potential regulatory overreach. 

The proposal is a clever play to keep the government onside. By giving the public a financial stake in AI’s success, Altman hopes to turn potential regulators into stakeholders. This move could help OpenAI avoid the kind of heavy-handed regulation that has already hit its competitors, like Anthropic, which has faced supply chain risks and export controls from the Pentagon.

But it’s not just about dodging regulation. The broader context is the growing public interest in capturing and redistributing the wealth generated by AI. From Trump’s 10 percent stake in Intel to Bernie Sanders’ calls for a 50 percent tax on AI stocks, there’s a clear push to ensure that the public benefits from these massive tech gains. Altman’s proposal might be a savvy way to stay ahead of the curve, but it also signals just how much the government is eyeing the AI gold rush.
