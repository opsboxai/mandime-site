Algae blooms, peeling paint, and a host of fixes from hydrogen peroxide to nanobubblers have made it hard to diagnose what’s wrong with the Reflecting Pool. Even Swimming Pool Steve, a YouTube pool expert with nearly 91,000 subscribers, is stumped.

Steve Goodale, the man behind Swimming Pool Steve, has been inundated with questions about the Lincoln Memorial Reflecting Pool. The pool, which turned green after a renovation, has become a source of frustration and confusion. Goodale, who has been in the pool business for decades, is no stranger to complex pool issues, but this one is particularly tricky.

"The mystery has only deepened as chunks of the newly installed lining have appeared to break off," Goodale explains. "There’s not enough information in terms of pictures, videos, water chemistry values. A natural pond, an open-air, clear-water environment, that’s what this thing is, there are so many moving parts here."

Goodale points out that pools are mercilessly complicated, and this one is monumental in size. The installation process involves a lot of technical points, including ambient conditions like rain, sun, humidity, and moisture control. If the material hasn’t bonded to the substrate properly, the entire system can fail.

The Department of Interior has been using nanobubblers, an ozone injection system, to try and fix the algae problem. In theory, it should work, but the results have been mixed. Goodale suggests that the water quality and the source of the water in the pool are crucial factors. "If this were my swimming pool, I would walk right up to it, dunk my little bottle into it and take a sample," he says. "I would test for phosphates, and it would tell me an awful lot about the nutrients available for algae to grow."

The cleanup and repair of the Reflecting Pool could be a costly endeavor for taxpayers. Goodale notes that the complexity of the issue means there are many potential causes and solutions, making it hard to pinpoint a single fix. "This is literally monumental in size. It would take a master class in technical execution to be able to work on this thing in a competent capacity."

In the end, the Reflecting Pool saga highlights the intricate nature of maintaining large bodies of water. Even with the best intentions, things can go awry, leaving experts like Goodale scratching their heads.
