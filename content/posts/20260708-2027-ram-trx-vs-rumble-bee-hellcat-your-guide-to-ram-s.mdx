If you thought the 2027 Ram TRX was the pinnacle of Ram’s performance game, think again. Enter the Rumble Bee Hellcat, a new contender that’s shaking things up in the supercharged pickup segment. Both trucks are powered by the same 777-horsepower Hemi V8, but they’re built for entirely different purposes.

The TRX is the ultimate off-roader, with ground clearance to spare, off-road tires, and Bilstein adaptive dampers to handle the roughest terrain. It’s built to conquer the desert, not the drag strip. Meanwhile, the Rumble Bee Hellcat is all about speed on the pavement. With a shorter wheelbase, a lower stance, and a stiffer suspension, it’s designed to corner and accelerate like a sports car, not a truck.

So, which one should you get? If you’re a desert rat who lives for the thrill of the trail, the TRX is your go-to. But if you’re more about speed and handling, the Rumble Bee Hellcat is the way to go. And if you can’t decide, you’ll have to wait a bit longer for the Rumble Bee to hit the market, while the TRX is already available for a cool $102,790. 

Byron Hurd breaks down the specs and the differences, but the real question is, which one suits your lifestyle better?
