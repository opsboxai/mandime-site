every couple of years, intermittent fasting resurfaces as a trendy diet. but according to experts, it's not just about weight loss. "intermittent fasting is not a weight-loss diet," says stacie stephenson, dc, cns, board member of the american nutrition association. "it's a really great tool."

so, what's the real deal with intermittent fasting? it's as simple as not eating for half the day. "for 12, 14 hours of your day, you’re not consuming anything other than water," explains stephenson. this approach isn't about losing weight or building muscle; it's about giving your body a break from constant calorie intake.

"resting your digestive system reduces total body inflammation, which leads to better health and longevity," says stephenson. this means more energy, balanced hormones, and stable blood glucose levels. essentially, it's like hitting a reset button on your body's core processes.

while intermittent fasting isn't a magic bullet for weight loss, it can indirectly help by regulating blood sugar. "when you start to control and reintegrate that system, you reignite insulin sensitivity," says colin robertson, phd, nutritionist, and exercise physiologist. this can lead to better overall health, but it's important to remember that weight loss still comes down to calories in and calories out.

one of the most surprising benefits of intermittent fasting is improved mental clarity. "when you're not digesting all the time, there's more energy available for your brain," notes stephenson. this was a personal revelation for me when i tried intermittent fasting a couple of years ago. i noticed i felt sharper mentally, and many others who've tried it reported similar experiences.

ultimately, intermittent fasting is a powerful tool for improving overall health, but it's not a silver bullet. if weight loss is your goal, you'll need to address your diet quality and quantity as well. "just addressing the timing is only one piece of the puzzle," says ashley koff, rd, nutrition course director for uc irvine’s susan samueli integrative health institute.

so, next time you're considering intermittent fasting, remember it's about more than just skipping a meal. it's about giving your body a break and improving your overall health.
