The 2027 Chevrolet Corvette Stingray just hit a major milestone: it's officially a 200-mph car. That's right, the base model Stingray can now hit 200 mph, a full six mph over its predecessor. And here's the kicker: it's the cheapest way to get into a 200-mph car, starting at just $73,495. 

But there's a catch. If you add the Z51 performance package, which adds downforce to the car, it lowers the top speed. Chevy hasn't disclosed exactly how much, but it's a significant enough drop to consider. 

The secret sauce behind this speed boost is the new 6.7-liter LS6 V-8 engine, which now cranks out 535 horsepower and 520 pound-feet of torque. This isn't just about speed; it's about the engineering prowess that goes into making a car that can hit these numbers. 

On a day like National Corvette Day, it's worth reflecting on how far the Corvette has come. The first production Corvette rolled off the line on June 30, 1953, with a top speed of about 105 mph. Today, it's more than doubled that. So, whether you're a die-hard Corvette fan or just someone who appreciates a good engineering feat, the 2027 Stingray is a car worth celebrating.
