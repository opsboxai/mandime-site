The Lexus SC 430 once got a bad rap, thanks in part to "Top Gear" and a few less-than-glowing reviews. But now, even those who once loathed its design are starting to see the light. My coworker Logan recently admitted to appreciating the SC 430's style, and he's not alone. Readers have chimed in with a list of cars that have grown on them over the years, from the late '90s/early '00s retromobiles like the last Ford Thunderbird to the 1990-1994 Lexus LS400, which was once dismissed as a "fancy Camry."

One of the most interesting transformations is the perception of the E63 BMW M6 Coupe. Upon its debut, many thought it looked awkward, but over the past decade, it's become a stunning GT in the eyes of many. This shift in opinion isn't just about aesthetics; it's about how our tastes evolve and how we come to appreciate the bold design choices that once seemed off-putting.

Another notable entry is the Nissan Juke, which was once dubbed the "frog car." Now, it's seen as a quirky, fun ride, especially in its GT-R engine variant. The R33 Skyline, once overlooked as the middle child, is now appreciated for its classic lines, standing out even more against its siblings, the R32 and R34.

These changes in perception show that car design, like fashion, is cyclical. What was once considered ugly can become a classic with the right perspective. So, the next time you see a car that doesn't quite hit your aesthetic, give it a second chance. You never know when it might grow on you.
