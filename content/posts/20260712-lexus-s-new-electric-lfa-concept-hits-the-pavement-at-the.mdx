Just under a year after its quiet debut in Monterey, the second-generation Lexus LFA concept has finally hit the pavement at the Goodwood Festival of Speed. This electric supercar, shrouded in camouflage, made its first public run, albeit a slow one, alongside its V-8-powered predecessors. The sound of those roaring engines likely drowned out any subtle sounds from the electric LFA, but the move itself is a big deal.

The LFA's appearance at Goodwood mirrors Toyota's strategy with the GR GT last year, suggesting a similar timeline for a full reveal. If history repeats itself, we could see the production version of the LFA in the coming months. This electric supercar is set to join the GR GT as a halo car, with the latter being marketed under the GR brand and sold through Lexus dealerships. It's a bold move by Lexus to have both gas and electric supercars in their lineup, catering to a wide range of enthusiasts.

While we're still in the dark about the LFA's exact specs, the fact that it's making waves at Goodwood is a clear sign that Lexus is serious about reviving the LFA name with a modern, electric twist. The LFA's legacy is strong, and this new iteration could be the perfect blend of nostalgia and innovation.
