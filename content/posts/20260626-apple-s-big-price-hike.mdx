Apple just hiked the price of its latest MacBook Pro by $200, and it's not just a minor bump. The 14-inch model now starts at $1,999, up from $1,799. MKBHD breaks down why this matters, and it's not just about the extra cash in your pocket.

The video dives into how this price increase affects the overall value proposition of the MacBook Pro. MKBHD points out that while the hardware improvements are notable, they might not justify the extra cost for everyone. The new model features a more powerful M2 Pro chip, better display, and improved cooling, but these upgrades come with a hefty price tag.

For power users and professionals who need top-tier performance, the extra $200 might be worth it. But for casual users, it's a different story. MKBHD suggests that the price hike could push some buyers towards alternative options, like Windows laptops or even older, less expensive Mac models.

The video also touches on how this pricing strategy could impact Apple's market share. With competitors like Dell and Lenovo offering powerful laptops at lower price points, Apple's decision to increase the price of its flagship laptop could make it less competitive in the broader market.

MKBHD's analysis is thorough, and he doesn't shy away from pointing out the potential downsides of Apple's pricing strategy. If you're in the market for a new laptop, this video is a must-watch to help you decide if the extra cash is worth the added performance.
