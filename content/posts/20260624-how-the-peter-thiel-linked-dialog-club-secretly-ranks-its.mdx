Leaked files reveal that Peter Thiel's private network, Dialog, grades its members based on wealth and fame, shaping who gets in, who sits where, and who's out. The trove of internal data, obtained by WIRED, includes personal information of nearly 200 prominent attendees, detailing everything from home addresses to political leanings.

Dialog, founded in 2006 by Thiel and data broker Auren Hoffman, is an exclusive club that convenes politicians, investors, entrepreneurs, and more for invitation-only retreats. The group assigns members grades of A, B, or C, with the "C" grade reserved for the most famous and influential. The leaked documents show that wealth and fame are key factors in these rankings, with some members being graded based on their Instagram following or the size of their assets under management.

For instance, actor Josh Brolin, who has never attended a Dialog retreat, was categorized as a VIP due to his fame from films like Avengers: Endgame. On the other hand, economist Tyler Cowen was initially denied a VIP rating because his field wasn't considered widely recognized by the average person. Dialog staff eventually overruled this decision, but the AI tool used to compile these dossiers highlights the group's meticulous approach to ranking its members.

The grades aren't just for show; they influence seating arrangements, event invitations, and even the cost of attendance. Members with lower grades can be disinvited from events for reasons like "Value Add Too Low" or "Poor Culture Fit." The group also tracks a "value-add" score of 1 to 4, averaged from staff ratings, and a "moderation tier" to determine who can moderate discussions or run workshops.

This system of ranking and grading members raises serious questions about the transparency and fairness of Dialog's operations. The leaked files show a highly curated and algorithmically driven approach to membership, where fame and wealth are the primary metrics. As the group continues to convene influential figures, the implications of this secretive ranking system are significant, especially given the power dynamics at play.

For guys into networking and high-stakes events, this is a glimpse into the inner workings of one of the most exclusive clubs out there. The takeaway? In the world of elite networking, it's not just about who you know, but how much money and fame you bring to the table.
