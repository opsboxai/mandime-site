80,000 fans packed into Levi's Stadium tonight, and it's clear that America is going all-in on soccer. The energy inside the stadium for the USA vs Bosnia match is electric, and it's a stark reminder of how much the sport has grown in the US. 

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is officially underway, and the Bay Area is at the heart of it. Six matches, including crucial knockout games, will take place at Levi's Stadium. The U.S. has already secured first place in Group D, setting up a highly anticipated Round of 32 match against Bosnia and Herzegovina on July 1. 

But this isn't just about the game on the pitch. The World Cup is about bringing communities together. From free viewing parties at Chase Center to the Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk, the entire region is getting in on the action. Even ABC News anchor Whit Johnson, who played college soccer at San Francisco State, is feeling the nostalgia of the 1994 World Cup, the last time the event was hosted in the Bay Area.

The pressure is on for Team USA, which hasn't won a knockout round game since 2002. With star Christian Pulisic back in the lineup, the team has a chance to change that narrative. But it's not just about the win; it's about the entire country embracing the beautiful game. The energy inside Levi's Stadium tonight is proof that soccer is here to stay in the US.
