Here’s a wild fact: nearly 650 workers at 87 park sites, including Glacier National Park, recently voted to join the National Treasury Employees Union (NTEU). This isn’t just a numbers game; it’s a response to deep cuts and layoffs that have hit the National Park Service hard.

President Trump’s second term saw a quarter of the agency’s employees laid off, along with spending freezes and threats of further layoffs. These austerity measures have left park workers feeling vulnerable and fighting for their jobs. Union representatives like Charlotte Graveline have seen firsthand how these actions have pushed workers to seek protection through labor unions.

The idea that park workers are "paid in sunsets" is a romantic notion, but it doesn’t cover the reality of low pay, unmanageable workloads, and high rent. These issues have been exacerbated by recent budget cuts and layoffs. The push for unionization isn’t just about job security; it’s about fighting for better working conditions, better pay, and a fairer treatment of dedicated employees who care deeply about the parks.

The movement is growing, with more workers in the Pacific West Region expected to vote on joining NTEU in July. This isn’t just about fighting back against cuts; it’s about ensuring that the people who keep our national parks running are treated with the respect and fairness they deserve.
