Andrew and Tristan Tate got picked up Saturday in Miami by US Marshals on rape and sex trafficking charges out of England. This isn't their first arrest on these charges, but the new indictment adds seven counts of rape and three of sex trafficking against Andrew, with British authorities now formally requesting extradition.

What's notable here isn't just the scale of the charges, but the speed at which the legal machinery actually moved. These guys spent years building a massive following across social media by selling a particular brand of misogyny packaged as self-improvement content. They got arrested in Romania in late 2022 on the original trafficking allegations, spent time in custody there, and faced mounting evidence from multiple jurisdictions. The US arrest signals that international law enforcement has finally coordinated enough to move toward prosecution.

The real story isn't the arrest itself—it's how two dudes managed to build a multimillion-dollar influence operation around openly contemptuous rhetoric about women, recruit thousands of paying followers into their "manosphere" ecosystem, and stay operational long enough for authorities to build cases across multiple countries. That took time. It also took the internet deciding they were worth paying attention to. The arrest in Miami is the visible punctuation mark, but the actual machinery behind it has been grinding for years.
