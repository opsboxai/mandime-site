At high-stakes meetings with the White House, Anthropic's CEO Dario Amodei has been replaced by cofounder Tom Brown. According to insiders, Amodei's reputation as a "weirdo" made him difficult to engage with, leading the Trump administration to prefer Brown's more cooperative approach.

The administration has been in multiple discussions with Anthropic, aiming to address concerns about the Claude Fable 5 AI model. Despite these talks, export controls on Anthropic's powerful models remain in place, following the National Security Agency's affirmation that guardrails could be bypassed.

Independent cybersecurity experts argue that guardrails are only a temporary solution, as skilled users and future AI models will likely find ways to disable them. The White House and Anthropic have been working on establishing criteria for proof that could alleviate the administration's concerns about jailbreaks.

A bipartisan group of lawmakers has also sent a list of questions to Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick, seeking clarity on the criteria for restoring public access to the model and the timeline for that decision. The letter demands responses by June 26, but it's unclear if the Commerce Department will meet this deadline.

Meanwhile, President Donald Trump has been preoccupied with the Lincoln Memorial reflecting pool, which has faced issues with algae blooms and sealant flaps. The administration has started erecting fencing around the pool and instructing the National Guard to detain anyone touching the water, citing regulations that prohibit damaging or defacing the structure.

The tension between Anthropic and the White House highlights the complex challenges in regulating advanced AI technologies. As the discussions continue, it remains to be seen how Anthropic will navigate these regulatory hurdles and whether they can regain public access to their powerful AI models.
