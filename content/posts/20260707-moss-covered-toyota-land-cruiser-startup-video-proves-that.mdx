A 1990 Toyota Land Cruiser, covered in moss and left to rot for years, still managed to fire up with just a little TLC. This isn’t just a testament to Toyota’s legendary reliability; it’s a damn miracle. 

Steve’s Place Down Under, an Aussie YouTube channel known for bringing old, forgotten vehicles back to life, found this Land Cruiser in a state of neglect. Despite being parked for an indeterminate amount of time, the engine turned over on the first try. The only real issue was some corrosion in the distributor, which Steve cleaned up and fixed. After a few attempts and a bit of fuel in the carburetor, the engine roared to life.

This isn’t just about a car starting; it’s about the enduring reputation of the Land Cruiser. These beasts have been known to survive everything from the harsh Australian outback to the grueling Dakar Rally. The fact that a 30-year-old model can still run after being left to the elements is a testament to its durability. 

But let’s not get too carried away. Running an engine and driving a car are two different things. The condition of the transmission, tires, and brakes remains to be seen. Still, hearing that old engine come to life is a damn good story, and it’s hard not to be impressed by the resilience of the Land Cruiser.
