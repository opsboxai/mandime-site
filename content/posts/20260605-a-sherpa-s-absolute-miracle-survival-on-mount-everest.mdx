Hillary Dawa Sherpa's survival on Mount Everest is nothing short of an absolute miracle. Left for dead on the mountain, she somehow made it back alive, a story that has captivated the Everest guiding community. Fred Dreier's account of her ordeal raises tough questions about the practices and ethics within the guiding industry. How could someone be left behind, and what does this say about the safety and support provided to Sherpas? This story is a stark reminder of the risks and the need for better protocols on the world's highest peak.
