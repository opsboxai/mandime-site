Driving a manual transmission car isn't just about mastering the clutch and gear shifts, it's a brain workout. According to a study by Tohoku University’s Institute of Development, manual driving stimulates the prefrontal cortex, the part of the brain responsible for memory, attention, and decision-making. This daily mental exercise can help keep your cognitive function sharp as you age.

Professor Ryuta Kawashima, who previously worked on Nintendo’s Brain Age puzzle games, led the research. He found that the physical and mental demands of operating a manual transmission provide a unique cognitive benefit that automatic transmissions simply can't match. In a world where manual transmissions are becoming increasingly rare, only 0.7 percent of new cars sold in the U.S. in 2024 came with a stick shift, this study offers a compelling reason to hang onto your manual car or seek out one of the 24 models still available with a manual transmission.

So, if you're looking for a way to keep your brain spry, consider hitting the road in a manual. It might just be the mental workout you need.
