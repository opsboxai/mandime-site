Claude can now autofill your passwords through 1Password's new browser integration, which means the AI can handle multi-step tasks like booking travel or managing accounts without you typing in credentials manually. This is genuinely useful for the mundane stuff, but it's also the kind of power that makes you want to know exactly what you're authorizing. The setup requires explicit permission each time, so you're not handing over blanket access to every password in your vault, but the model here is clear: AI assistants are moving from chat-only tools into active agents that can actually do things on your accounts. The friction between "I want AI to help me" and "I'm comfortable with AI having login access" is real, and how companies navigate that line matters more than the feature itself. If this becomes standard across password managers and AI platforms, we're looking at a pretty fundamental shift in how personal security and delegation work online. For now, it's opt-in and limited, which is the responsible play. Whether it stays that way depends entirely on how seriously Anthropic and 1Password take the stakes of credential access.
