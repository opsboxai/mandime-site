If you've ever wondered how to test the quality of a loaf, Donut Media put this one through the ringer. The bread trick they showcase is simple yet surprisingly effective. You slice the bread, spread butter on one side, and then stack the slices butter-side down. If the loaf holds together, it's a keeper. If it falls apart, it's a dud. 

This isn't just about the bread's structural integrity; it's about the texture and moisture content too. A good loaf should be dense enough to stick together but not so dense it's like eating cardboard. The video shows a few loaves failing the test, crumbling under the weight of their own buttery layers. 

Donut Media's take is a fun, hands-on way to evaluate your bread before you commit to a full sandwich. It's a quick, no-nonsense method that can save you from a soggy, disappointing meal. Next time you're at the bakery, give this trick a shot. It might just change the way you pick your bread.
