Chris Bumstead, aka "The Barbell Bros," just dropped a video that could change the way you approach your lat pulldowns. He introduces a new variation that targets your lats and upper back with a fresh twist. Instead of the standard seated pulldown, Bumstead demonstrates a standing variation that engages your core and stabilizer muscles, making it a more dynamic and effective exercise.

The setup is simple: you stand in front of the lat pulldown machine, grab the bar with a wide overhand grip, and pull it down to your chest while keeping your core tight and your feet shoulder-width apart. This standing variation not only hits your lats but also works your shoulders, traps, and even your abs, making it a full-body move.

Bumstead emphasizes the importance of maintaining proper form to avoid straining your lower back. He suggests starting with a lighter weight to get the hang of the movement and gradually increasing the load as you get comfortable. The key is to focus on pulling with your lats and not relying on momentum.

This variation is perfect for those looking to add some variety to their back routine and improve overall stability and strength. Whether you're a seasoned lifter or just starting out, Bumstead's demonstration is a must-watch. It's a game-changer for anyone looking to spice up their workout and see better results.
